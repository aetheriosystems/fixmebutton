## Before You Start

A screenshot is a picture of exactly what's on your phone screen. You can use screenshots to save recipes, capture error messages, share conversations, or keep proof of a purchase.

Every phone takes screenshots a little differently — but once you learn the button combo for your phone, it takes one second.

**What you'll need:**
- Your smartphone (iPhone or Android)
- Something on your screen you want to capture

> **Tip:** Screenshots save as photos. You can text them, email them, or post them just like any other picture from your camera.

![Phone screen showing a screenshot being taken](/images/guides/smartphones/screenshot-hero.jpg)

## Step 1: Identify Your Phone Type

Before you press any buttons, figure out which type of phone you have. The button combination is different for each one.

| If you have... | Look for... | Skip to... |
|----------------|-------------|------------|
| iPhone with Face ID (iPhone X or newer) | No home button on the front, just a screen | Step 2 |
| iPhone with Home button (iPhone SE, iPhone 8 or older) | A round button below the screen | Step 3 |
| Most Android phones (Google Pixel, Motorola, OnePlus) | Power button on the side, volume buttons | Step 4 |
| Samsung Galaxy phones | Power button + volume buttons (Bixby key on older models) | Step 5 |

**Common mistake:** Pressing the buttons in the wrong order or not pressing them at the same time. You must press both buttons together quickly — like snapping your fingers.

## Step 2: iPhone with Face ID (iPhone X and Newer)

If your iPhone doesn't have a round home button on the front, follow these steps:

1. Open whatever you want to capture on your screen
2. Press the **right side button** and the **volume up button** at the exact same time
3. Quickly release both buttons

You'll see the screen flash white and hear a camera shutter sound (if your phone isn't on silent). A small preview of your screenshot will appear in the bottom-left corner.

![iPhone Face ID screenshot buttons - side button + volume up](/images/guides/smartphones/screenshot-iphone-faceid.jpg)

> **Tip:** Tap the preview thumbnail in the corner to crop, draw, or add text to your screenshot before saving it. Swipe it left to dismiss it and save automatically.

**Common mistake:** Pressing the side button and the volume DOWN button instead of volume UP. On iPhones with Face ID, it must be the **volume up** button (the top one on the left side).

## Step 3: iPhone with Home Button (iPhone SE, 8 and Older)

If your iPhone has a round home button on the front:

1. Open whatever you want to capture
2. Press the **top button** (or right side button) and the **home button** at the exact same time
3. Quickly release both buttons

The screen will flash and you'll hear the shutter sound.

![iPhone with Home button screenshot - top button + home button](/images/guides/smartphones/screenshot-iphone-home.jpg)

## Step 4: Most Android Phones (Google Pixel, Motorola, OnePlus)

For most Android phones:

1. Open what you want to capture
2. Press the **power button** (on the side) and the **volume down button** at the exact same time
3. Hold for about 1 second, then release

The screen will flash and a preview will appear at the bottom. Some Android phones also show a "Screenshot saved" message.

![Android screenshot buttons - power + volume down](/images/guides/smartphones/screenshot-android.jpg)

> **Tip:** On most Android phones, you can also take a scrolling screenshot — which captures an entire webpage or long conversation. After taking a normal screenshot, tap the **"Capture more"** or **scrolling icon** (looks like a down arrow with lines) that appears next to the preview.

## Step 5: Samsung Galaxy Phones

Samsung phones use a slightly different method:

1. Open what you want to capture
2. Press the **power button** (right side) and the **volume down button** (left side) at the same time
3. Hold for about 1 second, then release

**Alternative Samsung methods:**
- Swipe the **edge of your palm** across the screen from right to left (called "Palm swipe to capture" — you may need to enable this in Settings → Advanced features → Motions and gestures)
- Say **"Hey Bixby, take a screenshot"** (on phones with Bixby)

![Samsung Galaxy screenshot gesture](/images/guides/smartphones/screenshot-samsung.jpg)

> **Tip:** Samsung phones have a "Smart Select" feature — swipe from the edge to open the Edge Panel, then choose Smart Select to capture just a specific area instead of the full screen.

## Where to Find Your Screenshots Afterward

**iPhone:**
1. Open the **Photos** app
2. Tap **Albums** at the bottom
3. Scroll down to **Media Types** and tap **Screenshots**
4. All your screenshots are in this folder

**Android:**
1. Open the **Photos** app (or **Gallery** app on Samsung)
2. Look for a **Screenshots** folder or album
3. On most Android phones: Photos app → Library → Screenshots

Or use your phone's **Files** app: Pictures → Screenshots.

![Finding screenshots in the Photos app](/images/guides/smartphones/screenshot-folder.jpg)

## Still Stuck?

- **Screen doesn't flash?** You might not be pressing both buttons at the exact same time. Try again, making sure you press and release them together — like a quick pinch.
- **Volume changes instead of taking a screenshot?** You're pressing the volume button first. Both buttons must go down at the same instant. Try resting your finger on the power button first, then quickly pressing volume down.
- **Button is broken?** 
  - **iPhone:** Go to Settings → Accessibility → Touch → AssistiveTouch (turn it on). A floating button appears. Tap it → Device → More → Screenshot.
  - **Android:** Swipe down from the top twice to open Quick Settings. Look for a **Screenshot** tile. If you don't see it, tap the pencil icon to add it. Many Android phones also let you take a screenshot by tapping the square overview button and looking for "Screenshot" at the bottom.
- **Screenshots are blank or black?** Some apps (like banking apps or Netflix) block screenshots for security reasons. This is normal — you can't screenshot those apps.
- **Want to capture a whole webpage?** On iPhone, take a screenshot, tap the preview, then tap "Full Page" at the top. On Android, use the scrolling screenshot feature mentioned in Step 4.
