## Before You Start

You click "Play," the Roblox window opens, and then... nothing. Or the game starts, freezes for a second, and vanishes. Roblox crashing or refusing to load is one of the most common problems players run into — and the good news is that most fixes take less than five minutes each.

This guide covers the five most effective fixes, from fastest to most thorough. Start at Step 1 and work your way down. Chances are, you'll be back in your favorite game before you reach the end.

**What you'll need:**
- Your computer, phone, or tablet
- A stable internet connection
- About 20 minutes of patience

> **Before you do anything else:** Check if Roblox itself is down. Go to [downdetector.com/status/roblox](https://downdetector.com/status/roblox) on your phone or another device. If you see a giant spike in outage reports, the problem isn't your device — Roblox's servers are having trouble, and all you can do is wait. This happens more often on weekends and during big update days.

## Step 1: Restart Your Device (It Actually Works)

It sounds too simple, but restarting your device is the single fastest fix for Roblox crashing. Here's why: over time, tiny errors pile up in your device's memory. Apps that you opened and closed leave behind fragments. A restart wipes all of that clean and gives Roblox a fresh start.

**How to restart properly — not just turn the screen off and on:**

**Windows PC or laptop:**
1. Click the **Start** button (the Windows icon in the bottom-left corner)
2. Click the **Power** icon
3. Choose **Restart** — NOT "Shut down" (Windows 10 and 11 have a "Fast Startup" feature that makes Shut Down act more like a deep sleep; Restart does a full, clean boot)
4. Wait for your computer to fully restart — don't rush to open Roblox the moment you see your desktop
5. Open Roblox and try again

**Mac:**
1. Click the **Apple menu** (top-left corner)
2. Select **Restart**
3. Uncheck "Reopen windows when logging back in" if that option appears
4. Click **Restart** to confirm

**iPhone or iPad:**
1. Press and hold the **power button** (and a volume button on Face ID iPhones) until the "slide to power off" slider appears
2. Slide to power off and wait 30 seconds
3. Press and hold the power button again until the Apple logo appears

**Android phone or tablet:**
1. Press and hold the **power button** until the power menu appears
2. Tap **Restart** (on some Androids you may need to tap "Power off" then turn it back on manually)
3. Wait for the device to fully restart

![A person pressing the restart button on a Windows PC Start menu](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** Pressing the power button to turn the screen off and on, thinking that counts as a restart. It doesn't. The screen turning off is just sleep mode — all those memory errors are still there when you wake it up. You need a full restart, which means the device actually shuts down and starts fresh.

## Step 2: Clear Roblox's Cache and Temporary Files

Roblox stores temporary files on your device to help games load faster. When these files get old or corrupted, they can cause crashes, infinite loading screens, and all sorts of weird behavior. Clearing the cache deletes these files — Roblox will create fresh ones the next time you play. You won't lose any game progress, Robux, or account data.

**Windows:**
1. Close Roblox completely (check your taskbar to make sure it's not still running)
2. Press the **Windows key + R** on your keyboard (a small "Run" window will pop up)
3. Type `%temp%` and press **Enter**
4. A folder full of temporary files will open. Press **Ctrl + A** to select everything
5. Press the **Delete** key on your keyboard
6. If some files say "in use" and won't delete, click **Skip** — that's normal
7. Now press **Windows key + R** again, type `%localappdata%`, and press **Enter**
8. Find the folder named **Roblox**, open it, and delete everything inside it
9. Restart your computer and try Roblox again

**Mac:**
1. Close Roblox completely
2. Open **Finder**, then click **Go** in the top menu bar
3. Select **Go to Folder** (or press **Command + Shift + G**)
4. Type `~/Library/Caches` and press **Enter**
5. Find the folder named **com.roblox.roblox** (or just **Roblox**), open it, and delete everything inside — but don't delete the folder itself
6. Now go back to **Go → Go to Folder** and type `~/Library/Logs/Roblox`
7. Delete everything inside that folder too
8. Restart your Mac and try Roblox again

**iPhone or iPad:**
1. Open the **Settings** app
2. Go to **General → iPhone Storage** (or iPad Storage)
3. Scroll down and tap **Roblox**
4. Tap **Offload App** — this removes the app but keeps your account data
5. After it's done, tap **Reinstall App**
6. Sign back into your Roblox account

**Android:**
1. Open the **Settings** app
2. Go to **Apps** (or "Apps & notifications")
3. Find and tap **Roblox**
4. Tap **Storage & cache**
5. Tap **Clear Cache** first, then tap **Clear Storage** (or "Clear Data")
6. Open Roblox and sign back in

![A Windows File Explorer window showing the Temp folder with all files selected and highlighted blue, about to be deleted](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** Deleting the Roblox app from your home screen (on mobile) without clearing the cache first. On iPhone and Android, simply deleting the app icon often leaves behind corrupted cache files that will cause the same problem when you reinstall. Always use the Settings app or Offload App option to clear data before reinstalling.

## Step 3: Close Background Apps and Lower Graphics Settings

Roblox might be crashing because your device is running out of memory (RAM) or processing power. Other apps running in the background — especially web browsers with lots of tabs, streaming apps, or other games — eat up resources that Roblox needs. And if your device is older or on the cheaper side, Roblox's default graphics settings might be too demanding.

**Fix 1 — Close everything that isn't Roblox:**

**Windows:**
1. Right-click an empty spot on your taskbar and select **Task Manager**
2. In the **Processes** tab, look at the list of running apps
3. Click on any app you don't need (especially browsers like Chrome, Edge, or Firefox), then click **End Task**
4. Do this for every non-essential app — especially anything with "web browser," "Spotify," "Discord," or "video" in the name
5. Now launch Roblox and test it

**Mac:**
1. Press **Command + Option + Escape** on your keyboard
2. A "Force Quit Applications" window will appear
3. Select any app you don't need and click **Force Quit**
4. Do this for every non-essential app, then try Roblox again

**iPhone/iPad and Android:**
1. Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and pause in the middle (or double-press the home button on older devices)
2. You'll see all your open apps as cards. Swipe each one up and off the screen to close it
3. Close everything, then open Roblox

**Fix 2 — Turn down Roblox's graphics quality:**

This fix applies to PC and Mac only. Mobile players can skip this since the mobile app automatically adjusts graphics.

1. Open Roblox and start ANY game (pick a simple one like "Natural Disaster Survival" or "Work at a Pizza Place" — something lightweight)
2. Once the game loads, look for the **Roblox icon** in the top-left corner of the screen (it looks like a gray square with a slanted square inside)
3. Click it to open the menu, then click **Settings**
4. Find **Graphics Mode** and switch it from **Automatic** to **Manual**
5. Click the **minus (–)** button next to "Graphics Quality" to lower it — try setting it to 3 or 4 bars instead of the max
6. Close the settings menu. The change takes effect immediately
7. Exit this game and try loading the game that was crashing before

![The Roblox in-game settings menu showing the Graphics Mode set to Manual with graphics quality lowered to 3 bars](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** Only closing browser tabs instead of fully quitting the browser program. Browsers like Chrome and Edge keep running in the background even when all tabs are closed — you'll see a little Chrome or Edge icon in the system tray (bottom-right corner of the screen, near the clock). Right-click it and choose **Exit** or **Quit** to fully close it before playing Roblox.

## Step 4: Update Your Graphics Driver and Operating System

Old graphics drivers are a leading cause of game crashes on computers. The graphics driver is the software that lets your computer talk to its graphics hardware. If it's out of date, Roblox (and other games) can crash, freeze, or show weird visual glitches. System updates matter too — Roblox is constantly updated, and sometimes it requires newer versions of Windows or macOS to run properly.

**Fix 1 — Update your graphics driver (Windows):**

1. Right-click the **Start** button and select **Device Manager**
2. Expand the **Display adapters** category by clicking the little arrow next to it
3. You'll see the name of your graphics hardware — usually **Intel**, **NVIDIA**, or **AMD**
4. Right-click the name and select **Update driver**
5. Choose **Search automatically for drivers**
6. If Windows finds an update, let it install, then restart your computer
7. If Windows doesn't find anything newer, visit the manufacturer's website directly:
   - **NVIDIA:** [nvidia.com/download](https://www.nvidia.com/download) — use "GeForce Experience" if you have it installed
   - **AMD:** [amd.com/support](https://www.amd.com/support)
   - **Intel:** [intel.com/support](https://www.intel.com/support) — or use the "Intel Driver & Support Assistant"

**Fix 2 — Update your operating system:**

**Windows 10 or 11:**
1. Open **Settings** (press **Windows key + I**)
2. Go to **Windows Update** (in Windows 10: **Update & Security → Windows Update**)
3. Click **Check for updates**
4. Install ALL available updates — especially any labeled "cumulative update" or "security update"
5. Restart your computer when prompted

**Mac:**
1. Click the **Apple menu** (top-left) and select **System Settings** (or System Preferences on older macOS)
2. Go to **General → Software Update**
3. If an update is available, click **Update Now** or **Restart Now**
4. Wait for the update to finish before trying Roblox

![Windows Update settings screen showing "Check for updates" button with updates available](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** Skipping graphics driver updates because "Windows Update" didn't find any. Windows Update often has older driver versions than what's available on the manufacturer's website. If Roblox is still crashing after Step 4, go directly to NVIDIA, AMD, or Intel's website and download the latest driver manually.

## Step 5: Do a Clean Uninstall and Reinstall of Roblox

If nothing else has worked, the problem is probably a corrupted installation — meaning some of Roblox's files got damaged or went missing. A regular uninstall sometimes leaves these broken files behind. A clean reinstall removes EVERY trace of Roblox so you can start completely fresh. You won't lose your account, Robux, friends, or game progress — all of that is saved on Roblox's servers.

**Windows — clean reinstall:**
1. Close Roblox completely
2. Open **Settings → Apps → Installed apps** (Windows 11) or **Settings → Apps → Apps & features** (Windows 10)
3. Search for **Roblox** in the list
4. Click the three dots next to Roblox and select **Uninstall** — confirm if asked
5. After uninstalling, press **Windows key + R**, type `%localappdata%`, and press Enter
6. Delete the entire **Roblox** folder if it still exists
7. Press **Windows key + R** again, type `%appdata%`, and press Enter
8. Look for a Roblox folder here too — delete it if found
9. Restart your computer
10. Go to [roblox.com](https://www.roblox.com), sign in, click any game, and press the green **Play** button — this will download a fresh copy of Roblox

**Mac — clean reinstall:**
1. Close Roblox completely
2. Open **Finder → Applications**
3. Drag the **Roblox** app to the **Trash** (or right-click and select **Move to Trash**)
4. Open **Finder**, click **Go → Go to Folder**, and type `~/Library/Preferences`
5. Find and delete ALL files that start with `com.roblox` — there may be several of them
6. Go back to **Go → Go to Folder** and type `~/Library/Caches`
7. Delete the `com.roblox.roblox` folder
8. Go to **Go → Go to Folder** and type `~/Library/Logs/Roblox`
9. Delete everything inside
10. Empty the **Trash**, restart your Mac, then go to roblox.com and reinstall

**iPhone/iPad — clean reinstall:**
1. Go to **Settings → General → iPhone/iPad Storage → Roblox**
2. Tap **Delete App** (not just Offload — this time you want a full deletion)
3. Restart your iPhone or iPad (hold power button, slide to power off, wait 30 seconds, turn back on)
4. Open the **App Store**, search for Roblox, and tap the download icon
5. Sign back into your account

**Android — clean reinstall:**
1. Go to **Settings → Apps → Roblox**
2. Tap **Storage & cache → Clear Data** (or Clear Storage)
3. Go back and tap **Uninstall**
4. Restart your phone or tablet
5. Open the **Google Play Store**, search for Roblox, install, and sign back in

![The Windows Apps & Features settings page showing Roblox selected with the Uninstall button visible](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** Skipping the "delete leftover folders" step on Windows or Mac and just clicking Uninstall. A normal uninstall leaves behind folders in AppData (Windows) or Library (Mac) that contain corrupted settings files. When you reinstall Roblox, it finds those old files and uses them again — bringing the crashing problem right back. Always manually delete the leftover folders after uninstalling.

## Still Stuck?

- **Does Roblox work on your phone but not your computer (or the other way around)?** Try playing on a different device for now. If Roblox works fine on one device but crashes on another, the problem is definitely something on that specific device — keep working through the steps for the device that's having trouble.

- **Does Roblox crash only in specific games, not all games?** Some Roblox games are way more demanding than others. Games with realistic graphics, lots of moving parts, or huge maps can overwhelm older devices even if simpler games run fine. Try lowering the graphics quality to the absolute minimum (Step 3, Fix 2) and closing ALL background apps before playing those heavier games.

- **Getting a specific error code?** Roblox sometimes shows error numbers when it crashes — like Error 277, Error 279, Error 610, or Error 529. Write down the exact number. Error 277 and 279 usually mean connection problems (check your internet). Error 529 usually means Roblox's servers are down. Error 610 usually means the game itself has a bug that the creator needs to fix — try a different game.

- **Did Roblox start crashing right after a Roblox update?** If dozens of other players are reporting the same problem on sites like Reddit (r/RobloxHelp) or the Roblox Developer Forum, it's likely a bug in Roblox itself — not your device. In these cases, Roblox usually releases a fix within a day or two. Check [downdetector.com/status/roblox](https://downdetector.com/status/roblox) to confirm.

- **Using a school or library computer?** Many schools block Roblox entirely through their network firewall. If Roblox won't even start installing, the school's network is probably blocking it. You'll need to play on a personal device on a different network.

- **Is your computer more than 5 years old?** Roblox's minimum requirements have slowly increased over the years. If your computer struggles with Roblox no matter what you try, the hardware might simply be below what Roblox needs to run smoothly. Check Roblox's official system requirements at [en.help.roblox.com](https://en.help.roblox.com/hc/en-us/articles/203312800) to see if your device meets them.
