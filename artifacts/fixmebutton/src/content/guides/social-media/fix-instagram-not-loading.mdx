## Before You Start

You open Instagram and see a blank white screen, a spinning loading circle that never stops, or the frustrating message **"Couldn't refresh feed."** Your feed won't update, Stories won't load, and you can't see anything new. Instagram is basically frozen.

This problem has many possible causes — a weak internet connection, a corrupted app cache, a conflict with the Facebook app, or even your phone's date and time being set wrong. This guide starts with the fastest, easiest fixes and works up to more involved solutions. Most people solve the problem by Fix 2 or 3.

**What you'll need:**
- Your phone (iPhone or Android)
- A stable internet connection (WiFi or mobile data)
- Your Instagram username and password (in case you get logged out)

> **Work through the steps in order.** Each fix builds on the last. Don't skip ahead — the early fixes solve most cases and take under a minute.

## Step 1: Check Your Internet Connection and Toggle Airplane Mode

Instagram needs a stable internet connection to pull in new posts and Stories. Even if other apps work fine, Instagram may be more sensitive to a weak or unstable connection. The "Couldn't refresh feed" error is most often a network issue in disguise.

**Fix 1 — Test and reset your connection**

1. Open your phone's web browser (Safari on iPhone, Chrome on Android) and try loading any website — like google.com or wikipedia.org. If web pages also won't load or load very slowly, your internet connection is the problem.
2. Swipe down from the top of your screen to open **Quick Settings** (Android) or **Control Center** (iPhone).
3. Tap the **Airplane Mode** icon to turn it ON. Wait 10 full seconds.
4. Tap the **Airplane Mode** icon again to turn it OFF. Your phone reconnects to the network fresh.
5. Open Instagram and check if your feed loads now.

**Fix 1b — Switch between WiFi and mobile data**

1. If you're on WiFi, turn WiFi OFF and try Instagram using your mobile data (cellular). If Instagram works on mobile data but not WiFi, your home WiFi or router is the problem — try restarting your router.
2. If you're on mobile data, turn mobile data OFF and connect to a WiFi network. If Instagram works on WiFi but not mobile data, you may have run out of mobile data for the month, or your cellular signal is too weak where you are.
3. Open Instagram and test your feed on each connection type.

**Fix 1c — Disable any VPN**

1. If you use a VPN app (Virtual Private Network) on your phone, open the VPN app and disconnect it. VPNs route your internet traffic through remote servers, and Instagram sometimes blocks or throttles traffic from VPN servers.
2. Once the VPN is off, force-close Instagram (swipe it away from your recent apps) and reopen it.
3. Check if your feed loads.

![An iPhone Control Center showing the Airplane Mode toggle being turned on](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People check their internet by opening another app like YouTube or TikTok, see it works, and assume their connection is fine. Different apps need different amounts of bandwidth and stability. Instagram pulls down a constant stream of images and videos — a connection that works fine for loading a single webpage might still be too weak for Instagram's feed. Always test with the web browser too, and try both WiFi and mobile data to rule out a network-specific problem.

## Step 2: Force-Close Instagram and Restart Your Phone

Apps can get stuck in a bad state where they're technically "open" but the internal processes that load your feed have frozen. Force-closing the app kills those stuck processes and gives Instagram a clean start. If that doesn't work, restarting your phone clears even deeper memory glitches.

**Fix 2 — Force-close the Instagram app**

1. **On iPhone (with Face ID):** Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and hold in the middle until you see the app switcher (all your open apps as cards). Swipe left or right to find the Instagram card, then swipe it UP and off the screen to close it.
2. **On iPhone (with Home button):** Double-press the Home button to open the app switcher. Swipe left or right to find Instagram, then swipe it UP and off the screen.
3. **On Android:** Tap the **Recent Apps** button (the square icon, usually at the bottom of the screen). Swipe left or right to find Instagram, then swipe it UP or tap the **X** to close it.
4. Wait 5 seconds, then tap the Instagram icon to reopen it. Check if your feed loads.

If force-closing once doesn't work, some users report needing to force-close and reopen Instagram **3-5 times in a row** before it finally breaks out of the loading loop. This is especially true if the app was stuck for a long time before you noticed.

**Fix 2b — Restart your phone**

1. **On iPhone:** Press and hold the side button and either volume button until the "slide to power off" slider appears. Slide to turn off. Wait 30 seconds, then press and hold the side button to turn it back on.
2. **On Android:** Press and hold the power button, then tap **Restart**. If your phone doesn't have a Restart option, tap **Power off**, wait 30 seconds, then press the power button to turn it back on.
3. Once your phone is back on, open Instagram and check your feed.

![An iPhone screen showing the app switcher with Instagram being swiped up to force-close it](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People tap the Home button or swipe up to go back to the home screen and think they've "closed" Instagram. That just minimizes the app — it keeps running in the background with the same frozen processes. You must fully force-close from the app switcher (remove it from your recent apps list) so the app actually restarts when you open it again. Also, some people restart their phone by turning the screen off and on — that's not a restart, that's just locking the screen. A real restart powers the phone completely off and on.

## Step 3: Clear Instagram's Cache and Data

Instagram stores temporary files (called "cache") on your phone to load images and videos faster. Over time, this cache fills up and can become corrupted — especially if you scroll through Instagram a lot. A corrupted cache can prevent the feed from loading, make images appear blank, or cause the "Couldn't refresh feed" error every time you open the app.

**Fix 3 — Clear cache on Android**

1. Open your phone's **Settings** app (the gear icon).
2. Scroll down and tap **Apps** (may be labeled "Apps & notifications" or "Application Manager" on some phones).
3. Scroll through the list and tap **Instagram**.
4. Tap **Storage** (or "Storage & cache").
5. You'll see how much storage Instagram is using. Tap **Clear Cache**. This is safe — it deletes temporary files only and does NOT delete your photos, posts, or account data.
6. Go back one screen and tap **Force Stop** to fully shut down the app.
7. Reopen Instagram and check if your feed loads.

**Fix 3 — Clear cache on iPhone (offload the app)**

iPhones don't have a "Clear Cache" button like Android does. Instead, you use the **Offload App** feature, which removes the app but keeps your documents and data. When you reinstall, the app starts fresh while keeping your login.

1. Open your iPhone's **Settings** app.
2. Tap **General** > **iPhone Storage**.
3. Scroll through the list and tap **Instagram**.
4. Tap **Offload App** (blue text). A confirmation message explains: "This will free up storage used by the app, but keep its documents and data."
5. Tap **Offload App** again to confirm.
6. Once it finishes, the Instagram icon on your home screen will be grayed out. Tap it — your iPhone will reinstall Instagram automatically.
7. Open Instagram. You may need to log in again, but all your photos and posts will still be there. Check if your feed loads.

If offloading doesn't work, try a full delete and reinstall (covered in Step 5).

![An Android phone Settings screen showing the Instagram app storage with Clear Cache button highlighted](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** On Android, people see the **Clear Data** button right next to **Clear Cache** and tap it, thinking it's the same thing. Clear Data deletes EVERYTHING — you'll be logged out, and the app resets completely as if you just installed it. Clear Cache is the safe one. Also, on iPhone, some people try to clear cache by deleting and reinstalling the app — but if you choose "Delete App" (red text) instead of "Offload App" (blue text), all app data is permanently removed. Offload is safer and faster.

> **Tip:** If Instagram's cache is over 500 MB on Android, that's way too much. A healthy cache should be 100-200 MB for a regularly used Instagram app. If you see numbers over 1 GB, your cache is bloated and almost certainly part of the problem. Make clearing the cache a monthly habit — your phone will run faster overall.

## Step 4: Check Date & Time, Data Saver, and Instagram Server Status

Three less obvious settings can completely break Instagram's ability to load your feed: your phone's date and time being wrong, data saver modes blocking background data, and Instagram's own servers being down. These are all quick to check.

**Fix 4a — Set your date and time to automatic**

Instagram uses your phone's clock to verify security certificates and sync your feed. If your date or time is manually set to the wrong value (even by a few minutes), Instagram can't communicate with its servers and throws the "Couldn't refresh feed" error.

1. **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** > **General** > **Date & Time**. Make sure **Set Automatically** is turned ON (green toggle).
2. **On Android:** Open **Settings** > **System** > **Date & time** (on Samsung phones: **Settings** > **General Management** > **Date and time**). Make sure **Set time automatically** (or "Use network-provided time") is turned ON.
3. If it was already on, toggle it OFF, wait 5 seconds, then toggle it back ON. This forces your phone to re-sync with the network time.
4. Force-close and reopen Instagram. Check your feed.

![An iPhone Date & Time settings screen showing Set Automatically toggle turned on](PLACEHOLDER)

**Fix 4b — Disable Data Saver modes**

Both Instagram and your phone have built-in "data saver" features that restrict how apps use internet data. These can be too aggressive and prevent Instagram from loading images and videos.

1. **Disable Instagram's own Data Saver:**
   - Open Instagram (if it loads enough to reach settings). Tap your profile icon (bottom-right), then the hamburger menu (three lines, top-right). Tap **Settings and privacy** > **Data usage and media quality**. Turn OFF **Data Saver** (Android) or **Use less cellular data** (iPhone).
   
2. **Disable your phone's system-wide Data Saver:**
   - **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** > **Cellular** > **Cellular Data Options** > **Data Mode**. Make sure **Low Data Mode** is OFF.
   - **On Android:** Open **Settings** > **Network & internet** > **Data Saver**. Turn OFF **Use Data Saver**. (On Samsung: **Settings** > **Connections** > **Data usage** > **Data Saver**.)

3. **On Android — Grant Instagram unrestricted data access:**
   - Go to **Settings** > **Apps** > **Instagram** > **Mobile data & Wi-Fi**. Turn ON **Unrestricted data usage** so Instagram can use data even when Data Saver is on for other apps.

4. Force-close and reopen Instagram. Check your feed.

**Fix 4c — Check if Instagram is down**

Sometimes the problem isn't your phone at all — Instagram's servers crash or have outages that affect millions of users.

1. Open your phone's web browser and go to **downdetector.com/status/instagram**. If you see a massive spike in reports, Instagram is having a server outage.
2. You can also check **X (Twitter)** — search for "Instagram down" and look for recent posts. If lots of people are complaining at the same time, it's a server-side problem.
3. If Instagram is down, there's nothing you can do on your phone except wait. Most outages last 30 minutes to 2 hours. You can still use Instagram through a web browser at **instagram.com** — sometimes the website works even when the app doesn't.

![The DownDetector website showing a spike in Instagram outage reports](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People manually set their phone's date and time to cheat in mobile games (like speeding up timers) and forget to switch it back. If your phone's date is off by days, weeks, or months, Instagram will completely break because the security certificates won't match. Setting date and time to automatic fixes this instantly. Also, some people check DownDetector, see "no problems," and assume Instagram is fine — but DownDetector only shows user-reported problems. A regional outage affecting your area might not show up yet if not enough people have reported it.

> **Tip:** Data Saver is a hidden culprit in a LOT of app loading problems — not just Instagram. If you routinely have problems with apps loading slowly, images not appearing, or videos not playing, try turning off Low Data Mode / Data Saver on your phone. These features were designed for people with very limited data plans, and they can be overly restrictive on modern apps.

## Step 5: Remove the Facebook App Conflict and Reinstall Instagram

Here's a surprising fix that has helped thousands of users: the Facebook app installed on your phone can conflict with Instagram and prevent it from loading. Because Meta owns both Facebook and Instagram, the apps share background processes and login systems. When those shared systems glitch, Instagram gets stuck in a loading loop that nothing else fixes.

**Fix 5a — Delete the Facebook app (and Messenger)**

1. **Delete the Facebook app:** On your home screen, press and hold the Facebook app icon, then tap **Remove App** (iPhone) or **Uninstall** (Android). Confirm the deletion.
2. **Delete the Messenger app** if you have it installed — same steps as above.
3. Restart your phone (power off, wait 30 seconds, power on).
4. Open Instagram. Does your feed load now?

If Instagram still doesn't load, go further:

5. With Facebook and Messenger still deleted, also **delete Instagram** (press and hold the Instagram icon > Remove App / Uninstall).
6. Restart your phone again.
7. Reinstall **only Instagram** from the App Store or Google Play Store. Do NOT reinstall Facebook or Messenger yet.
8. Open Instagram and log in using your **email or username and password** — do NOT use the "Continue with Facebook" or "Log in with Facebook" option.
9. Check if your feed loads. If it does, the Facebook app conflict was the problem.

Once Instagram is working, you can reinstall the Facebook and Messenger apps. Wait at least 30 minutes after Instagram is running smoothly, then reinstall them one at a time — install Facebook first, test Instagram, then install Messenger. If Instagram breaks again after reinstalling Facebook, you've confirmed the conflict. You may need to keep Facebook uninstalled or use Facebook through your web browser instead.

![An iPhone home screen showing the Facebook app being deleted by pressing and holding the icon](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People delete Facebook and Messenger but then use the "Log in with Facebook" button when signing back into Instagram. This re-links the accounts through the same broken shared system and brings the problem right back. Always log in with your Instagram username/email and password — keep Facebook out of the login process entirely.

**Fix 5b — The complete clean reinstall (if Facebook wasn't the issue)**

If deleting Facebook didn't help, or if you never had the Facebook app installed, do a full clean reinstall of Instagram:

1. **Delete Instagram** from your phone (press and hold the icon > Remove App / Uninstall).
2. **Restart your phone** — don't skip this. The restart clears leftover files that a simple delete leaves behind.
3. After the restart, open the **App Store** (iPhone) or **Google Play Store** (Android).
4. Search for "Instagram" and tap the **download/install button**. Make sure the developer is "Instagram from Meta" to ensure it's the official app.
5. Once installed, open Instagram and log in with your username/email and password.
6. If you don't remember your password, tap **Forgot password?** on the login screen. Instagram will send a reset link to your email or phone number. Reset your password, then log in.
7. Check if your feed loads.

![The App Store page for Instagram showing the download button](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People delete and reinstall Instagram without restarting their phone in between. The restart is essential — it clears the system-level cache and any leftover background processes that survive an app deletion. Without the restart, you might just reinstall the problem along with the app. Also, some people panic when they reinstall and don't immediately see all their photos — your account, photos, followers, and posts are all stored on Instagram's servers, not on your phone. As soon as you log in, everything comes back.

> **Tip:** If you've been using Instagram for years without ever clearing things out, a clean reinstall can make the app noticeably faster overall — not just fix the loading problem. The app accumulates junk files, old search histories, and cached data from accounts you don't even follow anymore. A fresh install is like spring cleaning for your Instagram app.

## Still Stuck?

- **Feed loads but won't show new posts — stuck on old content?** Instagram's algorithm sometimes gets stuck showing you the same posts over and over. Try interacting differently: like a few posts from accounts you haven't engaged with recently, save a post, or follow a new account. This can "unstick" the algorithm and refresh what appears in your feed. Also, tap the **Instagram** logo at the top-left of the home screen and select **Following** instead of "For You" — this shows only posts from accounts you follow in chronological order.

- **Instagram works on another device but not your phone?** Log into your Instagram account on a friend's phone, a tablet, or a computer at **instagram.com**. If Instagram loads perfectly on another device, the problem is definitely your phone — not your account. Focus on the phone-specific fixes: clear cache (Step 3), check date and time (Step 4a), and do the clean reinstall (Step 5b).

- **"Couldn't refresh feed" only appears on one specific account?** If you have multiple Instagram accounts, try switching to a different account (press and hold your profile icon, bottom-right, then tap the other account). If the other account works fine, the problem is tied to the specific account. Try logging out of the problem account and logging back in. If that doesn't work, the account may have been flagged or restricted — check the email associated with your Instagram account for any notices from Instagram.

- **Instagram was working fine until a phone system update?** Major iOS or Android updates sometimes break app compatibility. If Instagram stopped working right after a system update, check the App Store or Google Play Store for an Instagram update — the developer usually releases a compatibility fix within a day or two of major OS updates. If no update is available, the clean reinstall (Step 5b) usually resolves post-update glitches.

- **Feed loads but images and videos appear blank or won't play?** This is usually a storage space issue. Go to **Settings** > **General** > **iPhone Storage** (iPhone) or **Settings** > **Storage** (Android) and check how much free space you have. If you have less than 1 GB free, your phone doesn't have room to download Instagram's media. Free up space by deleting unused apps, old photos, or videos. After freeing at least 2-3 GB, force-close and reopen Instagram.

- **None of the above works — how do I contact Instagram?** Instagram doesn't offer live chat or phone support for regular users, but you can report the problem in the app if it loads enough to reach settings. Tap your profile > hamburger menu (three lines) > **Settings and privacy** > **Help** > **Report a Problem**. Describe exactly what's happening, include screenshots if possible, and submit. You can also report at **help.instagram.com** from a computer. While Instagram rarely responds individually, reports help them identify and fix widespread bugs. In the meantime, you can use Instagram through a web browser at **instagram.com** — the web version has almost all the same features.
