## Before You Start

You open TikTok, and instead of the endless scroll of videos you expect, you get a spinning loading circle that never stops, a black screen where videos should be, or the message **"Couldn't load videos."** Your For You Page (FYP) won't refresh, videos freeze halfway through, or the app just shows a blank white screen. TikTok is basically broken, and nothing you tap seems to help.

This is one of the most reported TikTok problems — and it's almost always fixable from your phone in under 15 minutes. The causes range from a bloated cache to a wrong date setting to a conflict with your phone's battery saver. This guide walks you through the fixes that real users on Reddit, TikTok's own support forums, and tech communities have confirmed work, starting with the fastest and easiest.

**What you'll need:**
- Your phone (iPhone or Android)
- A stable internet connection (WiFi or mobile data)
- Your TikTok login info (in case you get logged out)
- About 15 minutes

> **Work through the steps in order.** The first two steps fix the problem for most people. Save the full reinstall for last.

## Step 1: Check Your Internet Connection and Toggle Airplane Mode

TikTok streams video — lots of it — so it needs a fast, stable internet connection. Even if other apps seem fine, TikTok is more sensitive to weak or unstable WiFi and mobile data. A quick connection reset is the fastest fix and solves the problem for a surprising number of people.

**Fix 1 — Toggle Airplane Mode to reset your connection**

1. Swipe down from the top of your screen to open **Quick Settings** (Android) or **Control Center** (iPhone).
2. Tap the **Airplane Mode** icon to turn it ON. You'll see a little airplane icon appear in the status bar.
3. Wait **15 full seconds** — count slowly. This forces your phone to disconnect from all networks.
4. Tap the **Airplane Mode** icon again to turn it OFF.
5. Your phone will reconnect to WiFi and mobile data fresh. Open TikTok and check if videos play now.

**Fix 2 — Switch between WiFi and mobile data**

1. If you're on WiFi, swipe open **Quick Settings** / **Control Center** and turn WiFi OFF. Open TikTok using your mobile data (cellular). If TikTok works on mobile data but not WiFi, the problem is your home network — skip to Fix 3.
2. If you're on mobile data, turn mobile data OFF and connect to a trusted WiFi network. Open TikTok on WiFi. If it works there, your cellular signal is too weak or you've run out of mobile data for the month.
3. Test your feed on each connection type to narrow down the cause.

**Fix 3 — Restart your WiFi router**

If TikTok only breaks on WiFi, your router may need a refresh:
1. Unplug your WiFi router's power cord from the wall outlet — not just from the back of the router.
2. Wait **60 seconds** (set a timer — this waiting period really matters).
3. Plug the router back in and wait 2 minutes for all the lights to stabilize.
4. Reconnect your phone to WiFi and open TikTok.

**Fix 4 — Disable any VPN**

VPN apps (Virtual Private Networks) route your internet through faraway servers. TikTok sometimes blocks or slows traffic coming from VPN servers, or the VPN itself doesn't handle video streaming well.

1. If you use a VPN app on your phone, open it and tap **Disconnect** (not just "pause" — fully disconnect).
2. Force-close TikTok (swipe it away from your recent apps), then reopen it.
3. Check if your feed loads. If it does, your VPN was the problem. Try a different VPN server location next time you connect, or add TikTok to your VPN's "split tunneling" list (if available) so TikTok bypasses the VPN.

![An iPhone Control Center showing the Airplane Mode toggle being turned on](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People test their internet by opening Instagram or YouTube, see those work, and assume their connection is fine. Different apps use different amounts of bandwidth — TikTok's endless video feed needs a much steadier connection than loading a single Instagram post. Always test TikTok specifically after a connection change. Also, some people disconnect their VPN app but forget that certain antivirus apps (like Norton 360 or McAfee) include a built-in VPN that runs silently in the background. Check your security apps and disable any VPN features there too.

## Step 2: Force-Close TikTok, Clear the Cache, and Restart Your Phone

TikTok stores temporary files (called "cache") on your phone to make videos load faster. Over time, this cache fills up with old data from hundreds or thousands of videos you've scrolled past. When the cache gets too big — or when a single corrupted file sneaks in — videos stop loading, the FYP freezes, or the app shows a black screen. Clearing the cache is like taking out the trash — it doesn't delete your account, drafts, or favorites.

**Fix 1 — Clear TikTok's cache (in the app)**

TikTok has a built-in cache cleaner — it's the safest and fastest way to clear temporary junk:

1. Open TikTok and tap the **Profile** icon in the bottom-right corner.
2. Tap the **☰ (three-line menu)** in the top-right corner.
3. Tap **Settings and privacy**.
4. Scroll down and tap **Free up space**.
5. You'll see a screen showing how much space TikTok is using. Next to **Cache**, tap the **Clear** button.
6. Tap **Clear** again to confirm. TikTok may freeze for a second — that's normal.
7. Force-close TikTok (swipe it away from recent apps), then reopen it and check your feed.

> **Is your cache over 1 GB?** That's way too much — a healthy TikTok cache should be 200–500 MB even for heavy users. If you're seeing 2 GB or more, clearing the cache will likely fix your problem AND make TikTok noticeably faster.

**Fix 2 — Clear TikTok's cache through phone settings (Android)**

If TikTok won't open far enough to reach in-app settings, use your phone's system settings instead:

1. Open your phone's **Settings** app (the gear icon).
2. Tap **Apps** (or "Apps & notifications" or "Application Manager" — wording varies by phone brand).
3. Scroll down and tap **TikTok**.
4. Tap **Storage** (or "Storage & cache").
5. Tap **Clear Cache**. This is safe — it only deletes temporary files and does NOT delete your drafts, favorites, or account data.
6. Tap **Force Stop** to fully shut down TikTok.
7. Reopen TikTok and check if your feed loads.

> ⚠️ Do NOT tap **Clear Data** — that button is right next to Clear Cache on some Android phones. Clear Data wipes EVERYTHING: your login, your drafts, your settings. Clear Cache is the safe one.

**Fix 3 — Clear cache on iPhone (offload the app)**

iPhones don't have a "Clear Cache" button like Android. Instead, use the **Offload App** feature:

1. Open your iPhone's **Settings** app.
2. Tap **General**, then **iPhone Storage**.
3. Scroll through the list and tap **TikTok**.
4. Tap **Offload App** (blue text). A message explains: "This will free up storage used by the app, but keep its documents and data."
5. Tap **Offload App** again to confirm.
6. Once it finishes, the TikTok icon on your home screen will be grayed out. Tap it — your iPhone re-downloads TikTok automatically.
7. Open TikTok. You may need to log in again, but your drafts and favorites are usually preserved. Check if your feed loads.

**Fix 4 — Restart your phone completely**

If clearing the cache alone doesn't work, restart your phone to clear deeper system-level memory glitches:

1. **On iPhone (with Face ID):** Press and hold the side button and either volume button until the "slide to power off" slider appears. Slide to turn off. Wait 30 seconds, then press and hold the side button to turn it back on.
2. **On Android:** Press and hold the power button, then tap **Restart**. If no Restart option appears, tap **Power off**, wait 30 seconds, then press the power button to turn it back on.
3. Once your phone is back on, open TikTok and check your feed.

![The TikTok "Free up space" screen showing the Cache section with the Clear button](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People clear the cache once, see TikTok still isn't loading, and give up. Sometimes you need to clear the cache AND restart your phone AND force-close the app — in that exact order — before the fix takes hold. The restart is the key step that people skip: clearing the cache removes the bad files, but the restart clears the memory where TikTok was still trying to use those bad files. Also, on iPhone, some people tap **Delete App** (red text) instead of **Offload App** (blue text). Offload keeps your data; Delete removes everything permanently.

## Step 3: Check Date & Time, Data Saver, Battery Saver, and Restricted Mode

Four hidden settings can silently break TikTok — and most people never think to check them. A wrong date, an aggressive data saver, low power mode, or an accidentally-turned-on Restricted Mode can all prevent TikTok from loading videos.

**Fix 1 — Set your date and time to automatic**

TikTok uses your phone's clock to verify security certificates and sync content. If your date or time is off — even by a few minutes — TikTok can't talk to its servers and your feed stops loading. This happens more often than you'd think, especially if someone manually changed the date to cheat in a mobile game.

1. **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **General** → **Date & Time**. Make sure the **Set Automatically** toggle is turned ON (green).
2. **On Android:** Open **Settings** → **System** → **Date & time** (on Samsung phones: **Settings** → **General Management** → **Date and time**). Make sure **Set time automatically** (or "Use network-provided time") is ON.
3. If it was already on, toggle it OFF, wait 5 seconds, then toggle it back ON. This forces a fresh sync with the network time.
4. Force-close and reopen TikTok. Check your feed.

![An iPhone Date & Time settings screen showing Set Automatically toggle turned on in green](PLACEHOLDER)

**Fix 2 — Disable Data Saver modes (both TikTok's and your phone's)**

TikTok and your phone both have "Data Saver" features that restrict how apps use the internet. These can be too aggressive and block TikTok from loading high-quality video.

1. **Disable TikTok's own Data Saver:**
   - Open TikTok → **Profile** → **☰ menu** → **Settings and privacy**.
   - Scroll down to **Cache & Cellular** (or "Data Saver" on some versions).
   - Turn OFF **Data Saver**. This lets TikTok load videos at full quality.

2. **Disable your phone's system-wide Data Saver:**
   - **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **Cellular** → **Cellular Data Options** → **Data Mode**. Make sure **Low Data Mode** is OFF.
   - **On Android:** Open **Settings** → **Network & internet** → **Data Saver**. Turn OFF **Use Data Saver**. (On Samsung: **Settings** → **Connections** → **Data usage** → **Data Saver**.)

3. **On Android — give TikTok unrestricted data:** Go to **Settings** → **Apps** → **TikTok** → **Mobile data & Wi-Fi**. Turn ON **Unrestricted data usage** so TikTok works even when Data Saver is on for other apps.

**Fix 3 — Turn off Battery Saver / Low Power Mode**

When your phone's battery gets low, it automatically kicks into power-saving mode — which throttles performance, limits background data, and can prevent video-heavy apps like TikTok from loading properly.

1. **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **Battery**. Turn OFF **Low Power Mode** (the toggle should be gray, not green). You can also swipe open **Control Center** and tap the battery icon to turn it off.
2. **On Android:** Swipe down for **Quick Settings** and look for **Battery Saver** or **Power Saving**. Tap it to turn OFF. Or go to **Settings** → **Battery** → **Battery Saver** and turn it OFF.
3. Charge your phone to at least 20% if the battery is very low.
4. Force-close and reopen TikTok. Check your feed.

**Fix 4 — Check Restricted Mode**

TikTok's Restricted Mode filters out content that may not be appropriate for all ages. If it was turned on accidentally (or by a parent), it can sometimes glitch and prevent your feed from loading at all — especially if the filtering system gets confused.

1. Open TikTok → **Profile** → **☰ menu** → **Settings and privacy**.
2. Tap **Content preferences** (or scroll to find **Digital Wellbeing** on some versions).
3. Tap **Restricted Mode**.
4. If it's ON, turn it OFF. You may need to enter a passcode if one was set.
5. Force-close and reopen TikTok. Check if your feed loads.

![The TikTok Settings and privacy menu showing Cache & Cellular and Content preferences options](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People manually set their phone's date and time to cheat in mobile games (like speeding up timers or getting daily rewards early), then forget to switch it back. If your phone's date is off by days, weeks, or even months, TikTok's security certificates won't match — and the app silently fails to load anything. Setting date and time to automatic fixes this in seconds. Also, many people have Low Power Mode enabled 24/7 to save battery without realizing it cripples video apps. If you always keep Low Power Mode on, try turning it off for a few minutes while using TikTok — the difference can be dramatic.

## Step 4: Update TikTok, Free Up Phone Storage, and Check Server Status

Sometimes the problem isn't a setting — it's an outdated app, a phone so full it can't function, or an outage on TikTok's end. These three checks are quick but catch problems that the earlier steps can't fix.

**Fix 1 — Update TikTok to the latest version**

TikTok releases updates frequently — sometimes multiple times a week — to fix bugs and improve performance. An outdated version can break video playback, especially after TikTok changes something on their servers.

1. Open the **App Store** (iPhone) or **Google Play Store** (Android).
2. Tap the **Search** tab and type "TikTok."
3. Find TikTok in the results. If you see an **Update** button instead of "Open," tap it.
4. Wait for the update to install, then open TikTok and test your feed.
5. Also worth checking: go to your phone's app store, tap your profile icon, and look for any pending updates. Install ALL app updates — sometimes an iOS or Android system component update is needed for TikTok to work right.

**Fix 2 — Free up phone storage space**

TikTok needs free storage space to download and cache videos. If your phone is nearly full (less than 1-2 GB free), TikTok can't save temporary video files — and videos either won't load or will constantly buffer.

1. **Check your storage:**  
   - **iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **General** → **iPhone Storage**. Look at the bar at the top — it shows how much space you have left.  
   - **Android:** Open **Settings** → **Storage** (or **Device care** → **Storage** on Samsung). Check your available space.

2. If you have less than 2 GB free, it's time to clean house:
   - Delete old apps you haven't used in months (you can always reinstall them later).
   - Go through your photo gallery and delete duplicate photos, blurry shots, and old screenshots.
   - Check for large video files you've already shared or backed up — videos eat storage fast.
   - On iPhone, the **iPhone Storage** screen shows recommendations like "Review Large Attachments" and "Offload Unused Apps."
   - On Android, use **Files by Google** (free from the Play Store) to quickly find junk files, old downloads, and duplicate media.

3. After freeing at least 2-3 GB, restart your phone, open TikTok, and check your feed.

**Fix 3 — Check if TikTok's servers are down**

Sometimes the problem isn't your phone or your internet — TikTok itself is having issues. Server outages affect millions of users at once and usually last 30 minutes to a few hours.

1. Open your phone's web browser and go to **downdetector.com/status/tiktok**. If you see a massive spike in the outage graph (hundreds or thousands of reports in the last hour), TikTok is having server problems.
2. Search **X (Twitter)** or **Bluesky** for "TikTok down" and look at the most recent posts. If hundreds of people are complaining at the exact same time, it's a server-side issue.
3. Check **r/TikTok** on Reddit — during outages, pinned threads or popular posts usually confirm the problem.
4. If TikTok is down, there's nothing you can do on your phone. You can try using TikTok through a web browser at **tiktok.com** — sometimes the website works even when the app doesn't. Otherwise, just wait. Most outages resolve within 1-2 hours.

![The DownDetector website showing a spike in TikTok outage reports with the outage graph](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People see their storage bar is mostly full, think "I still have 1 GB left — that's plenty," and skip this step. TikTok needs CONTINUOUS free space to buffer videos as you scroll — 1 GB might fill up after just a few minutes of watching. You need at least 2-3 GB of breathing room. Also, checking Downdetector and seeing "no problems" doesn't always mean TikTok is fine — Downdetector only shows user-submitted reports. If a regional outage just started, it might not show up yet. Check multiple sources.

## Step 5: Log Out and Back In, Then Clean Reinstall TikTok

If you've tried everything above and TikTok still won't load, the problem is likely a corrupted app installation or a stuck account session. Logging out forces TikTok to refresh your account data from their servers. If that doesn't work, a clean reinstall wipes every trace of TikTok from your phone so nothing corrupted can carry over.

**Fix 1 — Log out of TikTok and log back in**

This sounds too simple, but logging out forces TikTok to discard your local session data and pull a fresh copy from TikTok's servers. If your local session got corrupted (which happens after app crashes or unexpected shutdowns), this can instantly fix loading problems.

1. Open TikTok → **Profile** → **☰ menu** → **Settings and privacy**.
2. Scroll all the way down and tap **Log out**.
3. Confirm by tapping **Log out** again.
4. Force-close TikTok completely (swipe it away from recent apps).
5. Reopen TikTok and tap **Log in**. Use your phone number, email, or linked account to sign back in.
6. Check if your feed loads now. This also resets your FYP algorithm slightly — so the first few videos may look different. That's normal.

> **Forgot your password?** On the login screen, tap **"Forgot password?"** or **"Use phone / email / username."** TikTok will send a verification code to your phone number or email. Use that code to reset your password and log back in.

**Fix 2 — Delete and clean reinstall TikTok (iPhone)**

1. From your home screen, press and hold the TikTok app icon.
2. Tap **Remove App** → **Delete App** → confirm by tapping **Delete**.
3. **Restart your iPhone** — don't skip this. Power off completely (Settings → General → Shut Down, or hold side + volume buttons), wait 30 seconds, then turn it back on.
4. Open the **App Store**, search for "TikTok" (developer: "TikTok Ltd."), and tap the download icon.
5. Once installed, open TikTok and log in. Check your feed.

**Fix 2 — Delete and clean reinstall TikTok (Android)**

1. From your home screen or app drawer, press and hold the TikTok app icon.
2. Tap **Uninstall** → confirm by tapping **OK**.
3. **Restart your phone** — don't skip this. Press and hold the power button, tap **Restart**.
4. After restarting, open the **Google Play Store**, search for "TikTok" (developer: "TikTok Pte. Ltd."), and tap **Install**.
5. Once installed, open TikTok and log in. Check your feed.

**Fix 3 — Check for phone system updates**

An outdated operating system can cause compatibility problems with the latest version of TikTok — especially if TikTok recently added new features that require a newer OS.

1. **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **General** → **Software Update**. If an update is available, tap **Download and Install**. Make sure your phone is plugged in or has at least 50% battery.
2. **On Android:** Open **Settings** → **System** → **System update** (on Samsung: **Settings** → **Software update** → **Download and install**). If an update is available, install it.
3. After the system update completes (your phone will restart), open TikTok and test your feed.

![An iPhone home screen showing the TikTok app being deleted by pressing and holding the icon with the Remove App menu visible](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People delete and reinstall TikTok without restarting their phone in between. The restart is THE most important part — it clears the system-level cache and any leftover background files that survive a simple app deletion. Without the restart, you might just reinstall the same corrupted state right back onto your phone. Also, some people panic after reinstalling and don't see their drafts. TikTok drafts are stored ON YOUR PHONE, not on TikTok's servers — they're deleted when you uninstall the app. If you have important drafts, save them to your camera roll before deleting TikTok (open the draft → tap **Save** or the download icon).

> **Tip:** If you've been using TikTok for years, a clean reinstall once or twice a year is actually good practice — it clears accumulated junk, resets glitchy settings you forgot you changed, and often makes the app noticeably faster. Think of it as spring cleaning for your TikTok.

## Still Stuck?

- **TikTok works on a friend's phone but not yours?** Log into your account on a friend's phone or a tablet. If TikTok loads perfectly on their device, the problem is definitely YOUR phone — not your account. Focus on the phone-specific fixes: clear cache (Step 2), check date and time (Step 3, Fix 1), free up storage (Step 4, Fix 2), and the clean reinstall (Step 5, Fix 2).

- **Videos load but the FYP only shows old content or videos you've already seen?** TikTok's algorithm sometimes gets stuck. Try interacting differently for a few minutes: like a few posts from accounts you don't normally engage with, save a video, search for a new topic, or follow a new account. This can "unstick" the algorithm. You can also tap and hold on a video you don't like and select **Not Interested** — this actively retrains your FYP.

- **TikTok was working fine until a phone system update?** Major iOS or Android updates sometimes temporarily break app compatibility. Check the App Store or Google Play Store for a TikTok update — the developer usually releases a compatibility fix within 24-48 hours. If no update is available yet, the clean reinstall (Step 5) often resolves post-update glitches.

- **"Couldn't load videos" only happens on ONE specific account, but other accounts work fine?** If you have multiple TikTok accounts, switch to a different one (Profile → tap your username at the top → switch account). If the other account loads fine, the problem is tied to your specific account — not your phone. Try logging out and back in (Step 5, Fix 1). If that doesn't work, your account may have been flagged or restricted. Check the email linked to your TikTok account for any notices, and contact TikTok support through **Profile → ☰ → Settings and privacy → Report a problem**.

- **Videos play but have no sound?** Check that your phone's silent mode switch (the physical mute toggle on iPhones) isn't turned on. Also check TikTok's own volume: while a video is playing, tap the screen and look for a speaker icon in the bottom-right or in the side menu. If it has an "X" or is crossed out, tap it to unmute.

- **TikTok uses too much storage even after clearing the cache?** Check for saved videos. Go to **Profile → ☰ → Settings and privacy → Free up space** and look at the **Downloads** section. Every video you've saved for offline viewing is stored here, and they can add up to gigabytes. Clear them if you don't need them anymore.

- **None of this works — how do I contact TikTok?** TikTok doesn't offer live chat or phone support for regular users, but you can report problems in the app. Go to **Profile → ☰ → Settings and privacy → Report a problem**. Select the topic that best matches your issue, describe exactly what's happening (include screenshots if possible), and submit. While TikTok rarely responds to individual reports, mass reports help them identify widespread bugs. In the meantime, you can use TikTok through a web browser at **tiktok.com** — the web version has most of the same features and often works even when the app doesn't.
