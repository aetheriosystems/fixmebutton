## Before You Start

You tap on a YouTube video, and instead of playing, you get a spinning loading circle that never stops, a black screen where the video should be, or the video starts and stops every few seconds to buffer. Maybe the YouTube app won't open at all — just a white screen with the red logo, then nothing. It's one of the most common YouTube complaints across Reddit, Apple Support Communities, and Google's own help forums, and it's almost always fixable from your phone.

The problem can come from a lot of places: a corrupted app cache, an over-aggressive data saver, a phone that's too full, a VPN silently blocking video traffic, or even your phone's date being set wrong (which breaks YouTube's security checks). This guide covers every fix that real users have confirmed works, starting with the fastest and easiest.

**What you'll need:**
- Your phone (iPhone or Android)
- A stable internet connection (WiFi or mobile data)
- Your Google account login info (in case you get logged out)
- About 15 minutes

> **Work through the steps in order.** The first step fixes the problem for most people. Save the clean reinstall for last — you probably won't need it.

## Step 1: Check Your Internet Connection, Switch Networks, and Disable Your VPN

YouTube streams video — a LOT of video — so it needs a fast, stable internet connection. Even if other apps like Instagram or TikTok work fine, YouTube is more sensitive to weak or spotty connections because it constantly downloads high-quality video. A quick connection reset is the fastest fix and solves the problem for a surprising number of people.

**Fix 1 — Toggle Airplane Mode to reset your connection**

1. Swipe down from the top of your screen to open **Quick Settings** (Android) or **Control Center** (iPhone).
2. Tap the **Airplane Mode** icon to turn it ON. You'll see a little airplane icon appear in the status bar at the top of your screen.
3. Wait **15 full seconds** — count slowly. This forces your phone to completely disconnect from all networks — WiFi, mobile data, and Bluetooth.
4. Tap the **Airplane Mode** icon again to turn it OFF.
5. Your phone reconnects to WiFi and mobile data fresh, with a new network session. Open YouTube and try playing a video.

**Fix 2 — Switch between WiFi and mobile data**

1. If you're on WiFi, swipe open **Quick Settings** / **Control Center** and turn WiFi OFF. Open YouTube using your mobile data (cellular). If YouTube works on mobile data but not WiFi, the problem is your home network — skip to Fix 3.
2. If you're on mobile data, turn mobile data OFF and connect to a trusted WiFi network. Open YouTube on WiFi. If it works there, your cellular signal is too weak or you've run out of mobile data for the month.
3. Test the same video on each connection type to narrow down the cause.

**Fix 3 — Restart your WiFi router**

If YouTube only breaks on WiFi, your router may need a refresh:

1. Unplug your WiFi router's power cord from the wall outlet — not just from the back of the router.
2. Wait **60 seconds** (set a timer — this waiting period really matters).
3. Plug the router back in and wait 2–3 minutes for all the lights to stabilize.
4. Reconnect your phone to WiFi and open YouTube.

**Fix 4 — Disable any VPN app**

VPN apps (Virtual Private Networks) route your internet traffic through faraway servers. YouTube sometimes blocks or throttles traffic coming from VPN servers, or the VPN itself doesn't handle video streaming well. This is especially common with free VPNs.

1. If you use a VPN app on your phone, open it and tap **Disconnect** (not just "pause" — fully disconnect).
2. Force-close YouTube (swipe it away from your recent apps), then reopen it.
3. Try playing a video. If it works, your VPN was the problem.
4. Try a different VPN server location next time you connect, or add YouTube to your VPN's "split tunneling" list (if available) so YouTube bypasses the VPN.

**Fix 5 — Check YouTube's built-in connection speed**

YouTube has a hidden feature called "Stats for Nerds" that shows exactly what's happening with your connection:

1. While a video is trying to play (even if it's buffering), tap the **gear icon (⚙️)** in the top-right corner of the video player.
2. Tap **Stats for nerds**.
3. Look at the **Connection Speed** line. YouTube recommends at least **500 Kbps** for basic quality and **5,000+ Kbps** (5 Mbps) for HD. If your speed is below 500 Kbps, your internet connection is the bottleneck — try switching to a different network or moving closer to your WiFi router.
4. Also check the **Buffer Health** line. If it stays at 0 seconds, YouTube can't preload any video — this usually means there's a network block (VPN, firewall, or parental controls).

![An iPhone Control Center showing the Airplane Mode toggle being turned on with the airplane icon highlighted](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People test their internet by opening Instagram or TikTok, see those work, and assume their connection is fine. YouTube streams much more data per second than most social media apps — a connection that loads a few Instagram photos fine might still choke on a 1080p YouTube video. Also, some people disconnect their VPN app but forget that certain antivirus apps (like Norton 360 or McAfee Mobile Security) include a built-in VPN that runs silently in the background. Check your security apps and disable any VPN features there too.

## Step 2: Force-Close YouTube, Clear the Cache, and Restart Your Phone

YouTube stores temporary files (called "cache") on your phone to make videos load faster. Over time, this cache fills up with thumbnails, partial video data, and search history from hundreds of videos. When the cache gets too big — or when a single corrupted file sneaks in — videos stop loading, the app shows a black screen, or you get a permanent spinning circle. Clearing the cache is like taking out the trash: it doesn't delete your account, subscriptions, or watch history.

**Fix 1 — Force-close the YouTube app**

Before clearing any data, make sure YouTube is actually closed — not just minimized:

1. **On iPhone (with Face ID):** Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and hold in the middle until you see the app switcher (all your open apps as cards). Swipe left or right to find the YouTube card, then swipe it UP and off the screen.
2. **On iPhone (with Home button):** Double-press the Home button. Swipe to find YouTube, then swipe it UP and off the screen.
3. **On Android:** Tap the **Recent Apps** button (the square icon or three-line icon, usually at the bottom of the screen). Swipe to find YouTube, then swipe it UP or tap the **X** to close it.
4. Wait 5 seconds, then reopen YouTube.

**Fix 2 — Clear YouTube's cache (Android)**

Android phones let you clear an app's cache directly through system settings:

1. Open your phone's **Settings** app (the gear icon).
2. Tap **Apps** (or "Apps & notifications" or "Application Manager" — wording varies by phone brand).
3. Scroll down and tap **YouTube**.
4. Tap **Storage** (or "Storage & cache").
5. Tap **Clear Cache**. This is safe — it only deletes temporary files. It does NOT delete your downloads, watch history, or account data.
6. Now tap **Force Stop** to fully shut down YouTube.
7. Reopen YouTube and try playing a video.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do NOT tap **Clear Data** — that button is right next to Clear Cache on some Android phones. Clear Data wipes EVERYTHING: your login, your downloaded videos, your settings. Clear Cache is the safe one.

**Fix 2 — Clear YouTube's cache on iPhone (Offload the App)**

iPhones don't have a "Clear Cache" button like Android. Instead, use the **Offload App** feature, which removes the app but keeps your documents and data:

1. Open your iPhone's **Settings** app.
2. Tap **General**, then **iPhone Storage**.
3. Scroll through the list and tap **YouTube**.
4. Tap **Offload App** (blue text). A message explains: "This will free up storage used by the app, but keep its documents and data."
5. Tap **Offload App** again to confirm.
6. Once it finishes, the YouTube icon on your home screen will be grayed out. Tap it — your iPhone re-downloads YouTube automatically.
7. Open YouTube. You may need to log in again, but your history and subscriptions are usually preserved.
8. Try playing a video.

> **Is your YouTube storage over 1 GB?** On the iPhone Storage screen, you can see exactly how much space YouTube is using. If the "Documents & Data" number is over 500 MB — or especially over 1 GB — your cache is bloated and almost certainly the problem. Offloading and reinstalling should clear this.

**Fix 3 — Restart your phone completely**

If clearing the cache alone doesn't work, restart your phone to clear deeper system-level memory glitches:

1. **On iPhone (with Face ID):** Press and hold the side button and either volume button until the "slide to power off" slider appears. Slide to turn off. Wait 30 seconds, then press and hold the side button to turn it back on.
2. **On Android:** Press and hold the power button, then tap **Restart**. If no Restart option appears, tap **Power off**, wait 30 seconds, then press the power button to turn it back on.
3. Once your phone is back on, open YouTube and test a video.

![The Android Settings screen showing the YouTube app info page with Storage, Clear Cache, and Force Stop buttons visible](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People clear the cache once, see YouTube still isn't working, and give up. Sometimes you need to clear the cache AND restart your phone AND force-close the app — in that exact order — before the fix takes hold. The restart is the key step that people skip: clearing the cache removes the bad files, but the restart clears the memory where YouTube was still trying to use those bad files. Also, on iPhone, some people tap **Delete App** (red text) instead of **Offload App** (blue text). Offload keeps your data; Delete removes everything permanently including downloads.

## Step 3: Check Video Quality, Data Saver, Battery Saver, and Date & Time

Four hidden settings can silently break YouTube — and most people never think to check them. A video quality setting that's too high for your connection, an aggressive data saver, low power mode, or a wrong date can all prevent videos from playing.

**Fix 1 — Manually lower the video quality**

If your internet is just barely fast enough, YouTube may be trying to play video at a quality your connection can't handle. Manually lowering the quality often stops buffering instantly:

1. While a video is trying to play, tap the video player to show the controls.
2. Tap the **gear icon (⚙️)** in the top-right corner of the video.
3. Tap **Quality**.
4. Instead of "Auto" or "Higher picture quality," tap a lower option like **480p** or **360p**. The video will look less sharp, but it should play smoothly without buffering.
5. If even 360p buffers, drop to **144p** to test. If 144p plays fine, your internet connection is the bottleneck.

> **Tip:** Once you find a quality that plays smoothly, you can leave it there — or try bumping it up one level at a time until you find the sweet spot. YouTube's "Auto" quality setting sometimes overestimates your connection speed and picks a quality that's too high.

**Fix 2 — Disable Data Saver modes (both YouTube's and your phone's)**

YouTube and your phone both have "Data Saver" features that restrict how apps use the internet. These can be too aggressive and block YouTube from loading high-quality video or even playing anything at all:

1. **Disable YouTube's own Data Saver:**
   - Open YouTube and tap your **profile picture** in the bottom-right corner.
   - Tap the **gear icon (⚙️)** in the top-right corner to open Settings.
   - Tap **Video quality preferences** (or "Data saving" on some versions).
   - Turn OFF **Data saving mode** (or set both "On mobile networks" and "On Wi-Fi" to "Higher picture quality").
   - Also check: on the same Settings screen, make sure **Playback in feeds** is NOT set to "Off" — this prevents videos from auto-playing as you scroll.

2. **Disable your phone's system-wide Data Saver:**
   - **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **Cellular** → **Cellular Data Options** → **Data Mode**. Make sure **Low Data Mode** is OFF.
   - **On Android:** Open **Settings** → **Network & internet** → **Data Saver**. Turn OFF **Use Data Saver**. (On Samsung: **Settings** → **Connections** → **Data usage** → **Data Saver**.)

3. **On Android — give YouTube unrestricted data:** Go to **Settings** → **Apps** → **YouTube** → **Mobile data & Wi-Fi**. Turn ON **Unrestricted data usage** so YouTube works even when Data Saver is on for other apps.

**Fix 3 — Turn off Battery Saver / Low Power Mode**

When your phone's battery gets low, it automatically kicks into power-saving mode — which throttles performance, limits background data, and can prevent video-heavy apps like YouTube from loading properly:

1. **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **Battery**. Turn OFF **Low Power Mode** (the toggle should be gray, not green). You can also swipe open **Control Center** and tap the battery icon to turn it off.
2. **On Android:** Swipe down for **Quick Settings** and look for **Battery Saver** or **Power Saving**. Tap it to turn OFF. Or go to **Settings** → **Battery** → **Battery Saver** and turn it OFF.
3. Charge your phone to at least 20% if the battery is very low.
4. Force-close and reopen YouTube. Try playing a video.

**Fix 4 — Set your date and time to automatic**

YouTube uses your phone's clock to verify security certificates and sync content. If your date or time is off — even by a few minutes — YouTube can't talk to its servers and videos won't play. This happens more often than you'd think, especially if someone manually changed the date to cheat in a mobile game or skip a time-based restriction:

1. **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **General** → **Date & Time**. Make sure the **Set Automatically** toggle is turned ON (green).
2. **On Android:** Open **Settings** → **System** → **Date & time** (on Samsung phones: **Settings** → **General Management** → **Date and time**). Make sure **Set time automatically** (or "Use network-provided time") is ON.
3. If it was already on, toggle it OFF, wait 5 seconds, then toggle it back ON. This forces a fresh sync with the network time.
4. Force-close and reopen YouTube. Try playing a video.

![The YouTube video player showing the Quality menu open with 480p selected and other resolution options visible](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People manually set their phone's date and time to cheat in mobile games (like speeding up timers or getting daily rewards early), then forget to switch it back. If your phone's date is off by days, weeks, or even months, YouTube's security certificates won't match — and the app silently fails to load anything. Setting date and time to automatic fixes this in seconds. Also, many people have Low Power Mode enabled 24/7 to save battery without realizing it cripples video apps. If you always keep Low Power Mode on, try turning it off while using YouTube — the difference can be dramatic. And for Data Saver: YouTube has its OWN data saver setting that's separate from your phone's. You need to check both.

## Step 4: Update YouTube, Free Up Phone Storage, and Check for Server Outages

Sometimes the problem isn't a setting — it's an outdated app, a phone so full it can't function, or an outage on YouTube's end. These three checks are quick but catch problems that the earlier steps can't fix.

**Fix 1 — Update YouTube to the latest version**

YouTube releases updates frequently — sometimes multiple times a week — to fix bugs and improve performance. An outdated version can break video playback, especially after YouTube changes something on their servers:

1. Open the **App Store** (iPhone) or **Google Play Store** (Android).
2. Tap the **Search** tab and type "YouTube."
3. Find YouTube in the results (developer: "Google LLC"). If you see an **Update** button instead of "Open," tap it.
4. Wait for the update to install, then open YouTube and test a video.
5. Also worth checking: go to your phone's app store, tap your profile icon, and look for any pending updates. Install ALL app updates — sometimes an iOS or Android system component update (like "Google Play Services" on Android) is needed for YouTube to work right.

**Fix 2 — Free up phone storage space**

YouTube needs free storage space to cache and buffer videos. If your phone is nearly full (less than 1–2 GB free), YouTube can't save temporary video data — and videos either won't load or will constantly buffer:

1. **Check your storage:**
   - **iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **General** → **iPhone Storage**. Look at the colored bar at the top — it shows how much space you have left.
   - **Android:** Open **Settings** → **Storage** (or **Device care** → **Storage** on Samsung). Check your available space.

2. If you have less than 2 GB free, it's time to clean house:
   - Delete old apps you haven't used in months (you can always reinstall them later).
   - Go through your photo gallery and delete duplicate photos, blurry shots, and old screenshots.
   - Check for large video files you've already shared or backed up — videos eat storage fast.
   - On iPhone, the **iPhone Storage** screen shows recommendations like "Review Large Attachments" and "Offload Unused Apps."
   - On Android, use **Files by Google** (free from the Play Store) to quickly find junk files, old downloads, and duplicate media.

3. Also check YouTube's own downloaded videos: Open YouTube → **Profile picture** → **Settings** → **Background & downloads**. Tap **Delete all downloads** to free up space taken by offline videos you saved and forgot about.

4. After freeing at least 2–3 GB, restart your phone, open YouTube, and test a video.

**Fix 3 — Check if YouTube's servers are down**

Sometimes the problem isn't your phone or your internet — YouTube itself is having issues. Server outages affect millions of users at once and usually last 30 minutes to a few hours:

1. Open your phone's web browser and go to **downdetector.com/status/youtube**. If you see a massive spike in the outage graph (hundreds or thousands of reports in the last hour), YouTube is having server problems.
2. Search **X (Twitter)** or **Bluesky** for "YouTube down" and look at the most recent posts. If hundreds of people are complaining at the exact same time, it's a server-side issue.
3. Check **r/youtube** on Reddit — during outages, pinned threads or popular posts usually confirm the problem.
4. If YouTube is down, there's nothing you can do on your phone. Try using YouTube through a web browser at **youtube.com** — sometimes the website works even when the app doesn't. Otherwise, just wait. Most outages resolve within 1–2 hours.

![The DownDetector website showing a spike in YouTube outage reports with the outage graph and problem indicators](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People see their storage bar is mostly full, think "I still have 1 GB left — that's plenty," and skip this step. YouTube needs CONTINUOUS free space to buffer videos as you watch — 1 GB might fill up after just a few minutes of HD video. You need at least 2–3 GB of breathing room. Also, checking Downdetector and seeing "no problems" doesn't always mean YouTube is fine — Downdetector only shows user-submitted reports. If a regional outage just started, it might not show up yet. Check multiple sources. And don't forget about YouTube's offline downloads — people save videos for offline viewing and forget about them. They can quietly eat up gigabytes of storage.

## Step 5: Log Out and Back In, Then Clean Reinstall YouTube

If you've tried everything above and YouTube still won't play videos, the problem is likely a corrupted app installation or a stuck account session. Logging out forces YouTube to refresh your account data from their servers. If that doesn't work, a clean reinstall wipes every trace of YouTube from your phone so nothing corrupted can carry over.

**Fix 1 — Log out of YouTube and log back in**

This sounds too simple, but logging out forces YouTube to discard your local session data and pull a fresh copy from YouTube's servers. If your local session got corrupted (which happens after app crashes, unexpected shutdowns, or switching between multiple Google accounts), this can instantly fix playback problems:

1. Open YouTube and tap your **profile picture** in the bottom-right corner.
2. Tap your **account name/email** at the top of the menu.
3. Tap **Manage accounts** (or "Switch account").
4. Tap **Remove from this device** next to your account, or tap **Sign out**.
5. Force-close YouTube completely (swipe it away from recent apps).
6. Reopen YouTube and tap **Sign in**. Use your Google email and password to sign back in.
7. Try playing a video.

> **Tip:** If you have multiple Google accounts on your phone, try switching to a different account and testing YouTube. If YouTube works on another account but not yours, the problem is tied to your specific account — not the app. You may need to check your YouTube account settings or restrictions.

**Fix 2 — Delete and clean reinstall YouTube (iPhone)**

1. From your home screen, press and hold the YouTube app icon until a menu appears.
2. Tap **Remove App** → **Delete App** → confirm by tapping **Delete**.
3. **Restart your iPhone** — don't skip this. Power off completely (**Settings** → **General** → **Shut Down**, or hold side + volume buttons), wait 30 seconds, then turn it back on.
4. Open the **App Store**, search for "YouTube" (developer: "Google LLC"), and tap the download icon.
5. Once installed, open YouTube and sign in. Try playing a video.

**Fix 2 — Delete and clean reinstall YouTube (Android)**

1. From your home screen or app drawer, press and hold the YouTube app icon.
2. Tap **Uninstall** → confirm by tapping **OK**.
3. **Restart your phone** — don't skip this. Press and hold the power button, tap **Restart**.
4. After restarting, open the **Google Play Store**, search for "YouTube" (developer: "Google LLC"), and tap **Install**.
5. Once installed, open YouTube and sign in. Try playing a video.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** If you have videos saved for offline viewing (YouTube Premium feature), they will be deleted when you uninstall the app. Before uninstalling, go to YouTube → **Profile picture** → **Settings** → **Background & downloads** to see what you have saved. Watch or finish anything important before you delete the app.

**Fix 3 — Check for phone system updates**

An outdated operating system can cause compatibility problems with the latest version of YouTube — especially if YouTube recently added new features that require a newer OS:

1. **On iPhone:** Open **Settings** → **General** → **Software Update**. If an update is available, tap **Download and Install**. Make sure your phone is plugged in or has at least 50% battery.
2. **On Android:** Open **Settings** → **System** → **System update** (on Samsung: **Settings** → **Software update** → **Download and install**). If an update is available, install it.
3. After the system update completes (your phone will restart), open YouTube and test a video.

**Fix 4 — Check for conflicting apps (Android especially)**

On Android, certain apps can interfere with YouTube's ability to play video. The most common culprits are:

1. **Third-party YouTube downloaders or ad-blockers** — apps like "YouTube Vanced" (discontinued but still installed on some phones), "NewPipe," or ad-blocking VPNs can conflict with the official YouTube app. Try uninstalling any third-party YouTube-related apps.
2. **Screen overlay apps** — apps that draw over other apps (like blue light filters, screen dimmers, or floating chat bubbles) can sometimes block YouTube's video player. Go to **Settings** → **Apps** → **Special access** → **Display over other apps** (or "Appear on top") and temporarily turn off any apps with this permission.
3. After disabling these, force-close and reopen YouTube, then test a video.

![An iPhone home screen showing the YouTube app being deleted by pressing and holding the icon with the Remove App and Delete App options visible](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People delete and reinstall YouTube without restarting their phone in between. The restart is THE most important part — it clears the system-level cache and any leftover background files that survive a simple app deletion. Without the restart, you might just reinstall the same corrupted state right back onto your phone. Also, some people panic after reinstalling and can't find their Watch Later playlist or Subscriptions. These are tied to your Google account, not your phone — they'll reappear as soon as you sign back in. Your offline downloads, however, are stored on your phone and WILL be deleted. Save anything important first.

> **Tip:** If you've been using YouTube for years without a clean reinstall, doing one every 6–12 months is actually good practice — it clears accumulated junk, resets glitchy settings you forgot you changed, and often makes the app noticeably faster. Think of it as spring cleaning for your YouTube app.

## Still Stuck?

- **YouTube works on a friend's phone but not yours?** Sign into your YouTube account on a friend's phone or a tablet. If YouTube plays videos perfectly on their device, the problem is definitely YOUR phone — not your account. Focus on the phone-specific fixes: clear cache (Step 2), check data/battery savers (Step 3), free up storage (Step 4, Fix 2), and the clean reinstall (Step 5, Fix 2).

- **Videos play but the YouTube app itself is slow, laggy, or crashes when scrolling?** This is usually a cache problem. Go straight to Step 2, Fix 2 — clear the cache through your phone's settings. If YouTube's storage is over 1 GB, clearing the cache will likely make the app noticeably faster.

- **Videos play with no sound?** Check that your phone's silent mode switch (the physical mute toggle on the side of iPhones) isn't turned on. Also check the video player itself: while a video is playing, tap the screen and look for a speaker icon with an "X" — tap it to unmute. On Android, check that your media volume is turned up (press the volume button while a video is playing, then tap the three dots and make sure the media volume slider is up).

- **YouTube was working fine until a phone system update?** Major iOS or Android updates sometimes temporarily break app compatibility. Check the App Store or Google Play Store for a YouTube update — Google usually releases a compatibility fix within 24–48 hours. If no update is available yet, the clean reinstall (Step 5) often resolves post-update glitches.

- **Videos play but the quality is stuck at 360p or lower even though you have fast internet?** Go to YouTube Settings (**Profile picture** → **Settings** → **Video quality preferences**) and make sure both "On mobile networks" and "On Wi-Fi" are set to **Higher picture quality**, not "Data saver." Also check your phone's data saver (Step 3, Fix 2). On some carrier plans, video streaming is automatically throttled to 480p — check with your mobile carrier if the problem only happens on mobile data.

- **You see the error "This video is not available" or "Video unavailable"?** This usually means the video was deleted, made private by the uploader, or is blocked in your country. It's not a problem with your phone. Try a different video to confirm.

- **YouTube works in a web browser (like Chrome or Safari) but not in the app?** This confirms the problem is with the app itself, not your account or internet. Go straight to Step 5 and do a clean reinstall. In the meantime, you can use **youtube.com** in your phone's web browser — it has most of the same features and often works even when the app doesn't.

- **None of this works — how do I contact YouTube?** YouTube doesn't offer live chat or phone support for regular users, but you can send feedback in the app. Tap your **profile picture** → **Help & feedback** → **Send feedback**. Describe exactly what's happening (include the device and OS version) and submit. While YouTube rarely responds to individual reports, mass reports help them identify widespread bugs. You can also post on the **YouTube Help Community** (support.google.com/youtube/community) where expert users and sometimes YouTube staff help troubleshoot.
