## Before You Start

You tap the blue Facebook icon on your phone, and instead of your news feed, you get a spinning loading wheel that never stops, a blank white or blue screen, or the app crashes back to your home screen the moment it opens. Maybe it loads but won't show new posts, or videos won't play, or you get the message **"Something went wrong."** Either way, you're locked out of Facebook and missing updates from friends and family.

This is one of the most reported Facebook problems — Reddit, Apple Discussions, and Meta's own help forums are full of users asking the same question. The good news: it's almost always fixable in under 15 minutes without losing your account or data. This guide walks you through five fixes, from the fastest to the most thorough. Most people solve this by Fix 1 or Fix 2.

**What you'll need:**
- Your phone (iPhone or Android)
- A stable WiFi or mobile data connection
- Your Facebook login email/phone and password (in case you get logged out)
- About 15 minutes

> **Work through the fixes in order.** The first two fixes solve the problem for most people. Save deleting and reinstalling the app for last.

> **Before troubleshooting your phone:** Check if Facebook itself is down. Go to [downdetector.com](https://downdetector.com) and search for "Facebook." If you see a massive spike in outage reports in the last hour, the problem is on Facebook's servers — not your phone. You'll just have to wait (most Facebook outages last under an hour). Also check Twitter/X — search "Facebook down" to see if others are reporting the same issue.

## Fix 1: Force-Close the App and Restart Your Phone

Apps can get stuck in a broken state where they're technically running but can't do anything useful. Force-closing the app kills it completely so it starts fresh next time. Restarting your phone goes a step further — it clears out temporary system glitches that can affect every app.

**Fix 1a — Force-close the Facebook app**

**On iPhone (with Face ID — iPhone X and newer):**
1. Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and pause in the middle. You'll see all your open apps as cards.
2. Swipe left or right to find the Facebook app card.
3. Swipe the Facebook card **up** and off the top of the screen. It should disappear.
4. Wait 5 seconds, then tap the Facebook icon to reopen it fresh.

**On iPhone (with Home button — iPhone 8 and older):**
1. Double-press the **Home button** below the screen. Your open apps appear as cards.
2. Find the Facebook card and swipe it **up** to close it.
3. Wait 5 seconds, then reopen Facebook.

**On Android:**
1. Swipe up from the bottom of the screen and hold (or tap the **recent apps** button — the square icon at the bottom).
2. Find Facebook in the list of recent apps.
3. Swipe it **up** or tap the **X** to close it.
4. Then go to **Settings → Apps → Facebook → Force Stop** to make sure it's completely killed.

**Fix 1b — Restart your phone**

If force-closing alone didn't fix it:
1. **iPhone:** Press and hold the side button and either volume button until "slide to power off" appears. Slide to turn off. Wait 30 seconds, then press the side button to turn it back on.
2. **Android:** Press and hold the power button → tap **Restart**. If you don't see Restart, tap **Power off**, wait 30 seconds, then press the power button to turn it back on.
3. After the restart, open Facebook and test it.

![An iPhone screen showing the app switcher with the Facebook app card being swiped up to force-close it](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People tap the Facebook icon, see it flash open and immediately crash, and keep tapping it over and over hoping it'll work. If the app crashes on launch, tapping it repeatedly won't help — every launch is a fresh attempt that's going to hit the same bug. Force-close it FIRST, then try once. If it still crashes, move to the next fix.

## Fix 2: Check Your Internet Connection and Toggle Airplane Mode

Facebook loads a LOT of data — your news feed, photos, videos, Stories, Reels, notifications, and ads. It needs a solid, fast internet connection. Even if other apps seem fine, Facebook might be the first one to fail on a weak connection because it's so data-hungry.

**Fix 2a — Switch between WiFi and mobile data**

1. If you're on **WiFi**, turn WiFi OFF (swipe down from top-right on iPhone, or swipe down twice on Android → tap the WiFi icon) and use mobile data instead.
2. Open Facebook. Does it work now?
   - **If yes:** Your WiFi is the problem. Restart your router (unplug 30 seconds, plug back in, wait 2 minutes). Also move closer to the router.
   - **If no:** Stay on mobile data for now and try the next check.

3. If you're on **mobile data**, try WiFi instead (if available). If Facebook works on WiFi but not mobile data, your cellular connection is too weak. Move to a spot with better signal (near a window or outside) or wait until you have a stronger signal.

**Fix 2b — Toggle Airplane Mode**

This forces your phone to disconnect from EVERYTHING (WiFi, cellular, Bluetooth) and reconnect fresh. It clears stuck network connections:

1. Swipe down to open your phone's **Control Center** (iPhone) or **Quick Settings** (Android).
2. Tap the **Airplane Mode** icon (it looks like an airplane ✈️) to turn it ON.
3. Wait **15 seconds**. Don't rush — you want the disconnect to fully register.
4. Tap Airplane Mode again to turn it OFF.
5. Wait for your WiFi or mobile data to reconnect (you'll see the icons reappear at the top of the screen).
6. Open Facebook and test it.

> **Common mistake:** Turning Airplane Mode on and immediately back off. That one-second toggle doesn't actually reset anything — your phone needs time to fully disconnect from the network. Always wait at least 15 seconds with Airplane Mode ON before turning it off.

## Fix 3: Clear Facebook's Cache and Data

Over weeks and months of scrolling, Facebook stores temporary files (called "cache") on your phone — images, video thumbnails, profile pictures, and other data to make the app load faster. When this cache gets too big or corrupted, it can cause the app to crash, freeze, or fail to load. Clearing it is safe and won't delete your account.

**Fix 3a — Clear Facebook cache on Android**

Android lets you clear app cache and data separately:

1. Go to **Settings → Apps** (or **Settings → Apps & notifications → See all apps**).
2. Scroll down and tap **Facebook**.
3. Tap **Storage & cache**.
4. First, tap **Clear Cache**. This removes temporary files ONLY — you won't lose your login, settings, or downloaded content.
5. Open Facebook and test it.
6. If it still doesn't work: go back to **Storage & cache** and tap **Clear Storage** (or **Clear Data**). **Warning:** This will log you out of Facebook and reset the app to its fresh-install state. You'll need to sign in again with your email/phone and password. Your account, posts, photos, and friends are all safe — they're stored on Facebook's servers, not your phone.

**Fix 3b — Offload or delete and reinstall the Facebook app on iPhone**

iPhone doesn't have a "clear cache" button for individual apps, but you can achieve the same thing:

**Option A — Offload the app (keeps your data and login):**
1. Go to **Settings → General → iPhone Storage**.
2. Scroll down and tap **Facebook** in the list.
3. Tap **Offload App** (NOT "Delete App"). Offloading removes the app but keeps your documents, data, and login information.
4. Confirm by tapping **Offload App** again.
5. After it finishes, tap **Reinstall App**. Facebook will download fresh, and you should still be logged in.
6. Open Facebook and test it.

**Option B — Delete and reinstall (more thorough):**
1. Press and hold the Facebook icon on your home screen until a menu appears.
2. Tap **Remove App** → **Delete App** → **Delete**. This removes everything.
3. Go to the **App Store**, search for "Facebook," and download it fresh.
4. Open Facebook and sign in with your email or phone number and password.
5. Test if it loads properly now.

> **Common mistake:** On Android, people tap "Clear Data" thinking it's the same as "Clear Cache." It's not. Clear Cache removes temporary files and keeps you logged in. Clear Data resets the app completely and logs you out. Always try Clear Cache first — it fixes most problems without the hassle of signing back in.

> **Tip:** Facebook's cache can balloon to several gigabytes over time. If your phone is running low on storage, clearing Facebook's cache can free up a surprising amount of space. The app will rebuild a fresh, clean cache as you use it.

## Fix 4: Update the Facebook App and Your Phone's Operating System

Running an old version of the Facebook app is a top cause of crashes and loading failures. Meta releases updates constantly — sometimes multiple times a week — to fix bugs, patch security holes, and add features. If your app is even a few weeks out of date, it might have bugs that Meta already fixed. Likewise, an outdated phone operating system can cause compatibility problems.

**Fix 4a — Update the Facebook app**

1. **iPhone:** Open the **App Store** → tap your profile picture in the top right → scroll down to see pending updates. Find Facebook and tap **Update**. Or pull down on the screen to refresh the list of available updates.
2. **Android:** Open the **Google Play Store** → tap your profile picture → **Manage apps & device** → **Updates available**. Find Facebook and tap **Update**.
3. If Facebook doesn't appear in the updates list, you're already on the latest version. But you can still check: in the App Store or Play Store, search for Facebook and open its store page. If you see **Update** instead of **Open**, there's a new version available.
4. After updating, open Facebook and test it.

**Fix 4b — Update your phone's operating system**

Facebook relies on your phone's system software to work. If your phone's OS is outdated, the Facebook app might not be compatible:

1. **iPhone:** Go to **Settings → General → Software Update**. If an update is available, tap **Download and Install**. Make sure your phone is plugged in or charged above 50%.
2. **Android:** Go to **Settings → System → System update** (or **Settings → Software update** on Samsung). Tap **Check for updates**. If one is available, download and install it.
3. After updating your phone's OS, restart your phone, then open Facebook.

**Fix 4c — Check for a Facebook beta program**

If you joined Facebook's beta testing program (you get early access to new features before the public), beta versions are less stable and more likely to crash:

1. **Android:** Open the **Google Play Store** → search for Facebook → scroll down to **"You're a beta tester"** section. If you see this, tap **Leave** to exit the beta program. Then uninstall and reinstall Facebook to get the stable public version.
2. **iPhone:** Open **TestFlight** (Apple's beta app). If Facebook appears in TestFlight, you're running a beta. Stop testing by opening Facebook in TestFlight and tapping **Stop Testing**. Then reinstall Facebook from the App Store.

> **Common mistake:** People have automatic app updates turned off to save mobile data or battery, and don't realize they're running a Facebook app that's several months old. Meta releases bug fixes constantly, and the version from 3 months ago may have known crashing issues that are already patched. Go to your phone's app store settings and make sure automatic updates are ON — or at least check manually once a week.

## Fix 5: Check Date & Time, Battery Saver, and Account Issues

If none of the simpler fixes worked, the problem might be with your phone's settings or your Facebook account itself. These are less common but can completely block Facebook from working.

**Fix 5a — Check your phone's date and time**

Facebook (like many apps) uses secure connections that check your phone's date and time. If your date/time is wrong — even by a few hours — Facebook might refuse to load:

1. **iPhone:** Go to **Settings → General → Date & Time**. Make sure **Set Automatically** is ON (green toggle). If it's already on, toggle it OFF, wait 5 seconds, then toggle it back ON to force it to re-sync.
2. **Android:** Go to **Settings → System → Date & time** (or **Settings → General management → Date and time** on Samsung). Make sure **Automatic date & time** and **Automatic time zone** are both ON. Toggle them off and back on to force re-sync.
3. Open Facebook and test it.

**Fix 5b — Disable battery saver and data saver modes**

Battery saver and data saver modes restrict background data and app refresh — which can break Facebook's ability to load content:

1. **On iPhone:** Go to **Settings → Battery**. If **Low Power Mode** is ON (yellow battery icon), tap the toggle to turn it OFF. Also check **Settings → Cellular** (or Mobile Data) — make sure Facebook's toggle is ON (green) so it can use cellular data.
2. **On Android:** Swipe down twice to open Quick Settings. Look for **Battery Saver** or **Power Saving** — if it's ON, tap to turn it OFF. Also go to **Settings → Apps → Facebook → Mobile data & Wi-Fi** (or **Data usage**) — make sure **Background data** is ON and **Unrestricted data usage** is enabled.
3. Also check Facebook's own data saver: open Facebook (if you can) → tap the **menu (☰)** → **Settings & privacy → Settings** → scroll to **Media** → make sure **Data Saver** is OFF. When Data Saver is on, Facebook loads lower-quality videos and images, which can sometimes cause loading problems.

**Fix 5c — Try logging in on a different device or browser**

This tells you whether the problem is with your account or just your phone:

1. Open a web browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge) on your phone or a computer and go to **facebook.com**.
2. Log in with your email/phone and password.
3. Does Facebook load normally in the browser?
   - **If yes:** The problem is specifically with the Facebook APP on your phone — your account is fine. The fixes above (clearing cache, reinstalling) should resolve it.
   - **If no:** (Can't log in at all, account suspended message, or same loading problems on every device) — the problem is with your Facebook ACCOUNT. You may have been logged out for security reasons, your account may be temporarily locked, or there may be a flag on your account. Go to [**facebook.com/hacked**](https://www.facebook.com/hacked) on a browser to check if your account has any security flags. Follow the on-screen instructions to verify your identity and restore access.

> **Common mistake:** People assume the app is broken and go through every fix in this guide — but the real problem is that their Facebook account was temporarily locked because someone tried to log in from an unusual location. Facebook sometimes locks accounts without sending a clear notification. If Facebook works on a browser but not in the app, AND you can log into the browser version, your account is probably fine. But if you can't log in anywhere — app or browser — check your account status at facebook.com/hacked.

## Still Stuck?

- **Facebook opens but the news feed won't load or refresh?** Try pulling down on the feed to refresh (place your finger on the middle of the screen and drag downward, then release). If that doesn't work, tap the **menu icon (☰)** → **Feeds** → select a different feed (like "Favorites" or "Friends"). If other feeds work but your main feed doesn't, the problem may be with Facebook's content ranking algorithm — wait an hour and try again. Also check if you're seeing posts in the "Most Recent" view: tap **Feeds** → **Most Recent** — sometimes the algorithm-based feed has issues while the chronological feed works fine.

- **Facebook crashes ONLY when you try to watch a video or a Reel?** This is often a video playback bug in the app. Try: go to your phone's **Settings → Facebook** (iPhone) or **Settings → Apps → Facebook** (Android) and check if there's a setting to disable in-app video autoplay. On Android, go to Facebook's in-app settings: **menu (☰) → Settings & privacy → Settings → Media → Autoplay** and set it to **Never**. If videos work with autoplay off, the bug is in the autoplay feature. Keep autoplay off until the next Facebook app update.

- **Getting a specific error message?** Write it down exactly. Common ones include: "Session Expired" (just log in again), "You're temporarily blocked from using this feature" (you triggered Facebook's spam detection — wait a few hours), "Error performing query" (server-side Facebook bug — wait it out), and "No internet connection" when you definitely have internet (clear cache — Fix 3). Google the exact error message for more specific solutions.

- **Facebook works on WiFi but not mobile data, or vice versa?** Go to your phone's settings and check if Facebook has permission to use data:
  - **iPhone:** Settings → Cellular (or Mobile Data) → scroll down to Facebook → make sure the toggle is ON (green).
  - **Android:** Settings → Apps → Facebook → Mobile data & Wi-Fi → make sure "Allow app while Data saver on" is ON.

- **Facebook was working fine until the most recent app update?** A bad update from Meta can break the app. Check the App Store or Play Store for Facebook's version history and recent reviews. If you see a flood of 1-star reviews from the last few days all mentioning crashes or loading problems, it's a bug in the update — and you need to wait for the next update from Meta. In the meantime, you can use Facebook in your phone's web browser at **facebook.com** (the mobile website works nearly identically to the app).

- **Tried EVERYTHING and Facebook still won't work on your phone?** There are a few last-resort options:
  1. **Use Facebook Lite:** Download **Facebook Lite** from the App Store or Play Store. It's a stripped-down, official Facebook app designed for older phones and slower connections. It uses less data, less storage, and is more stable. It doesn't have every feature, but it gets you back on Facebook.
  2. **Use the mobile website:** Go to **m.facebook.com** in your phone's browser (Chrome or Safari). You can even add it to your home screen as a shortcut: on iPhone, tap the Share icon → **Add to Home Screen**. On Android, tap the three-dot menu in Chrome → **Add to Home screen**.
  3. **Factory reset your phone:** This is extreme and should be a true last resort — it erases everything on your phone. Only do this if Facebook is mission-critical and nothing else works. Back up everything first.
