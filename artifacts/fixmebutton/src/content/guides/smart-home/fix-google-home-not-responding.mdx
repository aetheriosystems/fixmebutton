## Before You Start

You say "Hey Google," and nothing happens — no lights, no response. Or the four white dots light up like it's listening, but then it goes dark without answering. Maybe it answers but can't do anything useful, saying "Something went wrong" or "I can't connect to the internet right now." Either way, your Google smart speaker or display is ignoring you.

This is one of the most common problems with Google Nest and Google Home devices. The good news: it's usually not broken — it's just disconnected, confused, or stuck. This guide walks you through the fixes in order, from the two-second checks to a full factory reset. Most people solve this by Fix 1 or Fix 2.

**What you'll need:**
- Your Google Nest or Google Home device
- The Google Home app on your phone (free from the App Store or Google Play)
- Your WiFi password (you might need to reconnect)
- A paperclip (for older devices with a reset pinhole)
- About 10 minutes

> **Work through the fixes in order.** The first two fixes solve over 80% of "Hey Google not responding" problems. Don't jump to the factory reset unless nothing else works — it wipes everything.

> **Is the device brand new or recently moved?** If this is your first time setting it up, or you just moved it to a new room, make sure it's plugged into a working outlet. Google Nest devices don't have batteries — they need to be plugged in at all times. Check that the power cable is firmly connected at both ends (the device AND the wall adapter).

## Fix 1: Do the Quick Checks First

Before diving into deeper fixes, check these three things that people overlook constantly. Each one takes under 10 seconds and solves more problems than you'd think.

**Fix 1a — Is the microphone switch turned off?**

Every Google Nest speaker and display has a physical **microphone mute switch**. When this switch is on, the device CANNOT hear you — no matter how loudly you say "Hey Google."

1. Look at the side or back of your device for a small sliding switch. On the original Google Home and Google Home Mini, it's on the back. On the Nest Mini and Nest Audio, it's on the side.
2. If the switch shows **orange** (or the LEDs are glowing orange), the microphone is muted. Slide the switch to turn the microphone back on.
3. The orange lights will turn off, and the device may say "The mic is on."
4. Try saying "Hey Google" again.

> **Common mistake:** People brush against the microphone switch while cleaning, dusting, or moving the device and accidentally mute it. They spend 20 minutes troubleshooting before realizing the mic is off. Always check the orange light first — if you see orange, your device is muted. That's the entire problem.

**Fix 1b — Are you saying the right wake word?**

Google Assistant only responds to two wake words: **"Hey Google"** and **"OK Google."** It does NOT respond to just "Google" by itself.

1. Check that you're saying the full phrase — "Hey Google" or "OK Google." Just saying "Google" won't work.
2. Speak clearly at a normal conversational volume. Don't shout from across the room and don't whisper.
3. Wait for the four white dots to appear before giving your full command. Say "Hey Google... what's the weather?" with a tiny pause in between.
4. If you have a thick accent or speech difference, try speaking slightly slower — Google Assistant uses the same speech recognition for everyone and sometimes needs a beat longer to process.

**Fix 1c — Is the volume all the way down?**

Google might be responding — you just can't hear it.

1. Say "Hey Google, set volume to 50%" or "Hey Google, volume 5."
2. Alternatively, press the **volume up** button: on Nest Mini and Nest Audio, tap the top right corner. On the original Google Home, tap the right side of the top surface.
3. On a Nest Hub display, swipe up from the bottom of the screen and use the volume slider.
4. If the device responds to "set volume" but you couldn't hear it before, the volume was just too low (or muted entirely).

> **Common mistake:** Someone in your household turned the volume way down at night so notifications wouldn't wake them up — and forgot to turn it back up in the morning. Or a child played with the touch controls and accidentally set the volume to 0. Always check the volume before anything else.

## Fix 2: Power Cycle Your Google Nest Device

A power cycle (unplugging it completely and plugging it back in) clears temporary software glitches, dropped network connections, and stuck processes. This is the single most effective fix for an unresponsive Google device.

**Fix 2a — Unplug from the wall, wait, plug back in**

1. Unplug the power adapter from the **wall outlet** — not just the cable from the back of the device. The power adapter needs to lose power completely.
2. Wait **30 full seconds**. Count slowly — don't rush. You want all the internal capacitors to drain so the device does a true cold restart.
3. Plug it back into the wall outlet.
4. The lights will turn on — usually four white dots that spin or pulse. Wait for the device to fully boot up. On Nest speakers, you'll hear a startup chime. On Nest Hubs, the screen will show the Google logo, then the home screen.
5. Wait until the lights stop pulsing (usually 30-60 seconds), then try "Hey Google."

**Fix 2b — If power cycling didn't work, restart from the Google Home app**

If you can't reach the physical device (it's mounted or behind furniture):

1. Open the **Google Home app** on your phone.
2. Find your device in the list. Tap and hold its tile (or tap it once to open, depending on your app version).
3. Tap the **gear icon (⚙️)** in the top right corner to open device settings.
4. Tap the **three-dot menu (⋮)** in the top right.
5. Tap **Reboot**. Confirm if asked.
6. Wait about 60 seconds for the device to fully restart.

> **Common mistake:** People unplug the cable from the back of the device but leave the power brick connected to the wall. The power brick holds a small charge, so the device may not fully reset. Always unplug from the WALL OUTLET. If you just pop the cable out of the back of the Nest Mini and plug it right back in, you haven't truly power cycled it.

> **Tip:** If your Google device has been acting flaky for a while (occasional unresponsiveness, delayed answers, cutting out mid-response), a weekly power cycle can keep it running smoothly. It's the smart home equivalent of restarting your computer — quick, harmless, and effective.

## Fix 3: Check and Fix Your WiFi Connection

Google Nest and Home devices need a constant internet connection for almost everything — even setting a timer requires talking to Google's servers. If your WiFi is weak, your network password changed, or the device lost its connection, "Hey Google" will either be ignored or answered with an error.

**Fix 3a — Check if the device is connected in the Google Home app**

1. Open the **Google Home app** on your phone.
2. Look at your device in the list. If it says **"Offline"** in orange or gray text under the device name, it's not connected to WiFi.
3. If there's a message like **"Can't reach this device"** or **"Not available,"** the WiFi connection is broken.
4. Tap the device anyway — the app may show more information about why it's offline.
5. If the device shows as online in the app but still doesn't respond to voice, the WiFi is probably fine — the issue is something else. Skip to Fix 4.

**Fix 3b — Move the device closer to the router**

WiFi signals weaken significantly through walls, floors, and over distance:

1. Temporarily move your Google device to the same room as your WiFi router.
2. Power cycle it (unplug for 30 seconds, plug back in).
3. Test "Hey Google." If it works in that room, your original location has a weak WiFi signal.
4. For a permanent fix: move the device to a better spot, move your router to a more central location, or get a WiFi extender or mesh WiFi system (Google's own Nest WiFi mesh points work seamlessly).

**Fix 3c — Reconnect to WiFi through the Google Home app**

If you recently changed your WiFi password, got a new router, or changed your network name:

1. Open the **Google Home app**.
2. If your device shows as offline, you should see a prompt to **"Set up"** or **"Reconnect."** Tap it.
3. The app will guide you through reconnecting. Your device may play a sound that the app listens for to confirm it's the right one.
4. Select your WiFi network and enter the new password.
5. If your device doesn't show the reconnect prompt: tap the device tile → gear icon (⚙️) → **Device information** → **Wi-Fi** → **Forget this network**. Then go back to the main screen and tap **+** → **Set up device** → **New device** to set it up fresh on the new network.

> **Important:** Google Nest devices only connect to the **2.4 GHz band** on most routers. If you have a dual-band router that broadcasts separate 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz network names, make sure you connect to the 2.4 GHz one. The 5 GHz network won't work for Google Nest speakers or displays.

> **Common mistake:** People change their WiFi password and then update every phone and laptop in the house — but forget about smart home devices. Google speakers can't prompt you for the new password on their own. You have to go into the Google Home app and manually reconnect each device. If you changed your WiFi password or got a new router recently, this is almost certainly the problem.

## Fix 4: Check for Software Updates and Re-link Your Google Account

Sometimes the device needs a software update to fix bugs, or it's having trouble talking to your Google account.

**Fix 4a — Check for and install a firmware update**

Google Nest devices update automatically when idle, usually overnight. But sometimes an update gets stuck or the device hasn't been idle long enough to download it:

1. Make sure your device is plugged in, connected to WiFi, and not actively playing music or responding to commands.
2. Leave the device idle for at least **15-30 minutes**. Don't talk to it, don't cast to it, don't use its touch controls. Just let it sit.
3. During this idle time, the device should check for and install any pending updates automatically. You won't see a progress bar — it happens silently.
4. After 30 minutes, try "Hey Google" again.

You can also prompt a firmware check by voice if the device is partially working: say **"Hey Google, check for updates."** If it says no update is available, you're on the latest version.

**Fix 4b — Remove and re-add your Google account in the Home app**

If your Google account is having trouble syncing with the device:

1. Open the **Google Home app**.
2. Tap your **profile picture** (or initial) in the top right corner.
3. Make sure you're signed into the correct Google account — the one you used when you first set up your Google Home devices.
4. Tap **Assistant settings** → scroll down and tap **Your data in the Assistant**.
5. Check if Voice Match is set up correctly. If it says "Voice Match not set up," you may need to retrain it. Tap **Voice Match** → **Teach your Assistant your voice again** and follow the prompts.
6. As a more thorough fix: go back to the main Home app screen, tap the device tile → gear icon (⚙️) → **Remove device** (at the bottom). Then tap **+** → **Set up device** → **New device** to add it back fresh. This re-links your account without needing to factory reset.

> **Common mistake:** People have multiple Google accounts on their phone (work, personal, old one they don't use anymore) and accidentally set up their Google Home under the wrong account in the Home app. Switch between accounts in the Home app by tapping your profile picture — the active account shows a checkmark. Make sure you're on the right one.

> **Tip:** If you share your home with family members, ask them to also try saying "Hey Google." If the device responds to them but not to you, your Voice Match profile may be corrupted. Retrain it: Google Home app → profile picture → Assistant settings → Voice Match → retrain.

## Fix 5: Clear Assistant Data and Check Google's Server Status

If your device hears you (four white dots appear) but can't complete commands — or says things like "Something went wrong, try again in a few seconds" — the problem might be on Google's end or with your Assistant data.

**Fix 5a — Check if Google Assistant servers are down**

Before spending more time troubleshooting your device, make sure the problem isn't on Google's side:

1. Go to [**downdetector.com**](https://downdetector.com) and search for "Google Home" or "Google Assistant."
2. Look at the outage map and the report graph. If you see a massive spike in reports in the last hour, Google's servers are having issues — and the problem is NOT your device.
3. Also check Twitter/X or Reddit (r/googlehome) — search "Google Home down" to see if other users are reporting the same issue.
4. If Google's servers are down, you just have to wait. These outages usually last 30 minutes to a few hours, and Google fixes them on their end.

**Fix 5b — Delete your recent Assistant activity and retrain**

Sometimes your Assistant's understanding of your voice or preferences becomes corrupted:

1. Open the **Google Home app** → tap your profile picture → **Your data in the Assistant**.
2. Scroll down to **"Review your recent activity"** and tap it. This opens your Google Assistant history.
3. Look for any commands that were totally misunderstood or returned errors. Tap the **X** or **Delete** on those entries to remove them.
4. Go back and tap **"Delete all Assistant activity"** (you may need to scroll to find this option). This clears your Assistant history without affecting anything else.
5. Now retrain Voice Match: go to **Assistant settings** → **Voice Match** → **Teach your Assistant your voice again**. Complete the voice training (saying "Hey Google" and "OK Google" a few times) in a quiet room.
6. Test your device again.

> **Common mistake:** People delete their Assistant activity but skip retraining Voice Match. The activity history and the voice model are separate things. If your device isn't recognizing your voice well, deleting history alone won't help — you need to retrain the voice model too.

## Fix 6: Factory Reset Your Google Nest / Home Device

If nothing above worked, a factory reset wipes the device completely and lets you set it up from scratch — exactly like when you first took it out of the box. **This should be your last resort** because it erases all your settings, WiFi connections, Voice Match data, and linked services. You'll have to set everything up again.

**For Google Nest Mini (2nd gen) and Google Nest Audio:**

1. Find the **microphone mute switch** on the side of the device. Slide it to the OFF position — the LEDs will turn orange.
2. Press and **hold** the very center of the top surface (where the lights are). Keep holding.
3. After about **5 seconds**, the device will begin the reset process (the lights may change pattern).
4. Continue holding for about **10-15 more seconds** until you hear a chime or a voice confirming the reset.
5. Release the button. The device will restart and eventually say something like "Welcome to Google Home. To get started, download the Google Home app."
6. Open the Google Home app → tap **+** → **Set up device** → **New device** and follow the instructions to set it up fresh.

**For Google Home Mini (1st gen):**

1. Find the small **reset button** on the bottom of the device — it's a tiny circle etched into the base, near the power cord.
2. Press and hold this button for about **5 seconds**. The device will announce it's resetting.
3. Continue holding for about **10 more seconds** until you hear a confirmation chime.
4. Release and set up through the Google Home app.

**For Google Nest Hub and Nest Hub Max (displays with screens):**

1. On the back of the device, find both **volume buttons** (volume up and volume down).
2. Press and hold BOTH volume buttons at the same time for about **10 seconds**.
3. The screen will go black and show the Google logo, indicating the reset has started.
4. Release the buttons. Follow the on-screen setup instructions.

**For Original Google Home speaker:**

1. On the back of the device, find the **microphone mute button** (it's the only physical button on the back).
2. Press and **hold** this button for about **15 seconds**.
3. The device will announce it's resetting. Keep holding until you hear the confirmation.
4. Release and set up through the Google Home app.

> **Warning:** A factory reset removes EVERYTHING — your WiFi settings, linked music services (Spotify, YouTube Music, etc.), Voice Match profile, smart home device connections, Routines, and alarms. You will need to set up the device as if it's brand new. Only do this as a last resort.

> **Common mistake:** Letting go of the button too early. On the Nest Mini, people press the center for 5 seconds, see the orange lights, and think the reset is done. But the orange lights at 5 seconds are from the mic being muted — NOT the reset. You must hold the center for the FULL 15+ seconds until you hear the confirmation sound. The same applies to all Google devices — a reset takes longer than most people expect. Count it out: hold, wait for the initial response (about 5 seconds), then KEEP HOLDING for another 10 seconds.

## Still Stuck?

- **Device works after a restart but stops responding again later?** Your device might be on the edge of your WiFi range. Even if it connects initially, a weak signal causes it to drop off periodically. Try moving it closer to the router or add a mesh WiFi point. Google's own Nest WiFi system works especially well with Google Home devices.

- **Other Google devices in the house work fine, just this one?** Swap locations between the broken device and a working one. If the broken device stays broken in the new spot, the device itself may be failing. If the working device now has problems in the old spot, that location has a WiFi dead zone.

- **Device responds but says "Something went wrong" or "I can't connect to the internet right now" for every command?** This is almost always a WiFi issue — even if the device appears connected in the app, the signal may be too weak for the cloud processing Google Assistant needs. Run a speed test on your phone while standing next to the device (go to speedtest.net). You need at least 1 Mbps for basic commands. If you're below that, the problem is your internet, not the device.

- **Google Home is still under warranty?** Google Nest and Home devices come with a 1-year limited warranty. Go to **store.google.com** → scroll to the bottom and click **"Help"** → search for "warranty" to check if you're eligible for a free replacement or repair.

- **If the device is frozen on the four dots and won't respond to anything:** This is called the "LEDs of death" in the Google Home community. Try a power cycle first (Fix 2). If that fails, do a factory reset (Fix 6). If the factory reset doesn't work either — the dots just keep spinning forever — the device's firmware may be corrupted. In that case, contact Google Support directly through the Google Home app (tap your profile picture → Help & feedback → Contact us).

- **Device is more than 4-5 years old (original Google Home from 2016-2017)?** The original Google Home speaker was discontinued and no longer receives feature updates, though it still gets security updates. If it's becoming increasingly unreliable, it may be time for a replacement. A new Google Nest Mini costs around $30-50 and has better microphones, faster processing, and a more reliable WiFi chip.

- **A specific smart home command isn't working ("turn on the lights," "set the thermostat") but other commands work?** The problem is with that specific smart home device or service, not your Google speaker. Check if the light bulb, thermostat, or plug is connected in its own app. In the Google Home app, tap the smart device, go to its settings, and try **"Reconnect account"** for the service (like Philips Hue, Nest, or SmartThings).

- **Still nothing after a factory reset?** At this point, the device may be physically broken. Common failures include: the power port becoming loose (try wiggling the power cable — if the device flickers, the port is damaged), the microphones failing (a hardware issue — the device can connect to WiFi but can't hear you at all), or the internal WiFi chip burning out. If it's more than 3-4 years old, replacement is usually more cost-effective than repair.
