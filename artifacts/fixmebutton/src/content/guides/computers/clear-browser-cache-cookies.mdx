## Before You Start

Clearing your browser cache and cookies fixes many common problems: websites that won't load properly, login pages that keep looping, old versions of pages showing up, and error messages like "502 Bad Gateway" or "This page isn't working."

**What you'll need:**
- Your computer with any web browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari, or Firefox)
- A few minutes — you'll need to log back into websites afterward

> **What's the difference?** **Cache** is saved copies of images and pages — clearing it frees up space and forces sites to load fresh versions. **Cookies** are small files that remember your logins and preferences — clearing them signs you out of most websites but fixes account-related glitches.

## Step 1: Open the Clear Browsing Data Menu

The fastest way to reach the settings in any browser is with a keyboard shortcut:

**Chrome, Edge, or Firefox:**
Press **Ctrl + Shift + Delete** (Windows) or **Command + Shift + Delete** (Mac)

**Safari (Mac):**
Press **Command + Option + E** to clear cache, or go to **Safari** menu → **Settings** → **Privacy** → **Manage Website Data**

> **No keyboard?** For Chrome/Edge: click the three dots (⋮) in the top-right corner → **Settings** → **Privacy and security** → **Delete browsing data**. For Firefox: click the three lines (☰) → **Settings** → **Privacy & Security** → **Clear Data**.

## Step 2: Choose Your Time Range

In the popup window that appears after using the keyboard shortcut, you'll see a "Time range" dropdown at the top.

| Option | What it does | When to use it |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Last hour** | Clears only the past 60 minutes | Just installed something, or a site broke recently |
| **Last 24 hours** | Clears today's activity | A problem started this morning |
| **Last 7 days** | Clears the past week | You've had issues for a few days |
| **Last 4 weeks** | Clears the past month | The problem has been around a while |
| **All time** | Clears everything | You want a completely fresh start |

**For a full reset, choose "All time."** This is the most thorough option and fixes the most problems.

**Common mistake:** Choosing "Last hour" when the problem has existed for days. The cache files causing your issue are probably older than one hour. Start with "All time" for troubleshooting.

## Step 3: Select What to Clear

You'll see checkboxes for different types of data. Here's what each one means and whether to check it:

**Essential (check these):**
- **Cached images and files** — This is the cache. Always check this. It forces websites to load fresh versions.
- **Cookies and other site data** — Signs you out of websites but fixes login problems, broken pages, and shopping cart errors.

**Optional (check based on your situation):**
- **Browsing history** — Your list of visited sites. Check this only if you want to clear your history for privacy. Does NOT affect website functionality.
- **Download history** — The list of files you've downloaded. Safe to clear; won't delete the actual files.

**Do NOT check these unless you know why you need to:**
- **Passwords and other sign-in data** — This deletes all your saved passwords. Only check this if you're giving away or selling your computer, or specifically troubleshooting password-saving issues.
- **Autofill form data** — Deletes saved addresses, phone numbers, and credit card info. Only check this if autofill is giving wrong information.

> **The minimum effective combo:** Check "Cached images and files" AND "Cookies and other site data." These two together fix 90% of website problems.

## Step 4: Clear the Data

1. Make sure your checkboxes are set correctly (at minimum: cache and cookies)
2. Click the **Delete data** button (Chrome/Edge) or **Clear Now** (Firefox)
3. Wait a moment — a large cache may take a few seconds to clear
4. Close and reopen your browser completely

**On Safari:** After clearing, quit Safari completely (Command + Q) and reopen it for the changes to take full effect.

## Step 5: Verify the Fix

1. Reopen your browser
2. Go to the website that was giving you trouble
3. Sign in again (you were signed out when cookies were cleared — this is normal)
4. Try whatever wasn't working before — can you log in? Does the page load correctly?

> **It worked!** If the problem is fixed, great — you're done. If not, try the next step below.

**If the problem persists after clearing cache and cookies, try one more thing:**

1. Open a **Private** or **Incognito** window (Ctrl + Shift + N in Chrome/Edge, Command + Shift + N in Safari)
2. Visit the website in that private window
3. If it works there but not in your normal window, the issue is with a **browser extension** — go to your extensions page and disable them one by one to find the culprit

## Still Stuck?

- **Specific website still broken?** The website itself might be having problems. Check downdetector.com or the company's social media to see if others are reporting issues.
- **Same problem on multiple browsers?** If clearing cache doesn't help on Chrome AND Edge AND Firefox, the issue might be your internet connection or computer. Try restarting your router or computer.
- **Can't remember your password after clearing cookies?** Use the "Forgot password" link on the login page. Most sites will send a reset link to your email.
- **Clearing cache didn't free up much space?** Browser cache alone rarely takes up more than 1-2 GB. If you need more free space, look at your Downloads folder and empty the Recycle Bin/Trash instead.
