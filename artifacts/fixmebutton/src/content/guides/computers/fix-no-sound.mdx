## Before You Start

No sound coming from your computer? Don't worry — this is almost always fixable in a few minutes. This guide walks you through the most common causes, from the simplest to the trickier ones.

**What you'll need:**
- Your computer
- If using external speakers: the speakers and their cable
- If using headphones: your headphones

> **Work through the steps in order.** Each step fixes the problem for most people — you probably won't need all of them.

## Step 1: Check the Obvious Stuff First

Before diving into settings, rule out the simple things that cause most "no sound" problems.

**Fix 1 — Is the volume muted?**
1. Look at your screen. Is there a speaker icon with an **X** next to it in the bottom-right corner (Windows) or top-right corner (Mac)?
2. **Windows**: Click the speaker icon in the taskbar. If there's an X, click it to unmute. Slide the volume bar to at least 50%.
3. **Mac**: Click the Control Center icon (two sliders, top-right) and look under **Sound**. Make sure the volume slider isn't all the way to the left.

**Fix 2 — Are external speakers turned on and plugged in?**
1. If you use external speakers, check they're plugged into a power outlet and turned on.
2. Check the speaker cable is firmly plugged into the computer's headphone jack (usually a green circle) or USB port.
3. Many speakers have a volume knob on them — make sure it's turned up.

**Fix 3 — Are headphones plugged in?**
If headphones are plugged into your computer, sound goes to the headphones, not the speakers. Unplug them and listen again.

> **Common mistake:** Many keyboards have a mute button (often on the top row, with a speaker icon). Accidentally pressing it mutes everything — even if the on-screen volume looks fine. Press it again to unmute.

## Step 2: Pick the Right Sound Output Device

Your computer might be sending sound to the wrong place — like HDMI to a monitor that doesn't have speakers.

**Windows:**
1. Click the **speaker icon** in the bottom-right corner of the taskbar
2. Click the small **arrow (>) or the output name** next to the volume slider
3. You'll see a list of output devices. The correct one is usually called **Speakers**, **Realtek Audio**, or your speaker brand name
4. Click the correct device to select it

![The Windows sound output menu showing multiple device options](PLACEHOLDER)

**Mac:**
1. Click the **Apple menu** (top-left) → **System Settings** → **Sound**
2. Under **Output**, you'll see a list of devices
3. Click the one you want — usually **Internal Speakers** for built-in speakers, or your speaker brand name

> **Tip:** If you see "HDMI" or your monitor's name selected, sound is being sent to your monitor, not your speakers. Switch to "Speakers" or "Internal Speakers."

## Step 3: Restart the Audio Service (Windows)

Sometimes Windows' sound system gets stuck. Restarting it often fixes the problem.

1. Press the **Windows key** on your keyboard and type **Services**
2. Click the **Services** app that appears (it has a gear icon)
3. In the list that opens, scroll down and find **Windows Audio**
4. Right-click **Windows Audio** and select **Restart**
5. Wait 5 seconds, then try playing sound again

![The Windows Services window with Windows Audio highlighted](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** Opening Services shows a big, confusing list. The only one you need is "Windows Audio." Don't change anything else in this window.

## Step 4: Check the App's Own Volume Settings

Sometimes the problem isn't your computer — it's just one program.

1. Open the app where you expect sound (YouTube in your browser, Spotify, a video player, a game)
2. Look for a **volume control inside the app**:
   - **YouTube/Web videos**: Check the speaker icon on the video player itself. Click it if it shows an X.
   - **Spotify**: Look for the volume slider in the bottom-right corner
   - **Zoom/Meet/Teams**: Check the microphone and speaker icons in the meeting controls
3. Also check your **browser tabs**: In Chrome or Edge, right-click a tab and look for **Mute site**. If the tab is muted, sound won't play from that website.

> **Tip:** Try playing sound from a different app. If Spotify is silent but YouTube works, the problem is in Spotify, not your computer.

## Step 5: Update or Reinstall Your Sound Driver (Windows)

Sound drivers are the software that lets Windows talk to your speakers. A bad or missing driver can kill sound completely.

1. Right-click the **Start button** (Windows logo, bottom-left) and select **Device Manager**
2. Find **Sound, video and game controllers** and click the arrow to expand it
3. Look for your sound device — it usually has "Realtek," "High Definition Audio," or "Intel" in the name
4. Right-click it and select **Update driver**
5. Choose **Search automatically for drivers**
6. Windows will look for a new driver and install it if found

If updating doesn't work:
1. Right-click the sound device again and select **Uninstall device**
2. **Do NOT check** the box that says "Delete the driver software" — just uninstall
3. Restart your computer. Windows will reinstall the driver automatically on startup.

![Device Manager showing the Sound, video and game controllers section](PLACEHOLDER)

> **Common mistake:** People sometimes uninstall the wrong device. Only uninstall items under "Sound, video and game controllers" — leave everything else alone.

## Step 6: Test on Mac with CoreAudio

On a Mac, sound problems are usually fixed by steps 1 and 2. But if you're still stuck, reset the Mac's sound system.

1. Open **Terminal** (find it in Applications → Utilities, or search with Spotlight — press **Cmd+Space** and type "Terminal")
2. Type this command exactly and press Enter:
   ```
   sudo killall coreaudiod
   ```
3. Type your Mac password (you won't see the letters appear — that's normal) and press Enter
4. Wait 5 seconds. The sound system will restart itself.

> **What this does:** CoreAudio is the program that handles all sound on a Mac. Killing and restarting it clears any glitches — it's like restarting just the sound part of your computer.

## Still Stuck?

- **No sound from one specific program?** Try reinstalling that program. If it's a web browser issue, try a different browser.
- **Sound is crackly or distorted?** This is different from "no sound." Check that your speaker cable isn't damaged and isn't wrapped around power cables, which can cause electrical interference.
- **Using Bluetooth speakers or headphones?** Bluetooth adds its own layer of potential problems. Try unpairing and re-pairing: go to **Settings → Bluetooth**, find your device, click **Forget/Remove**, then pair it again.
- **Sound worked yesterday but not today?** A recent Windows update might have changed your sound settings. Go to **Settings → Windows Update → Update history** and look for any updates installed around the time the problem started. You can try uninstalling the most recent update.
- **Still nothing?** Try a different pair of headphones or speakers to rule out broken hardware. If different speakers work, your original speakers or cables may be damaged.
