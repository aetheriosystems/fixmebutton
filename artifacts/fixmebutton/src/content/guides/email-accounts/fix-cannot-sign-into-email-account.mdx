## Before You Start

You know your password. You're typing it carefully. You've checked Caps Lock is off. But your email still won't let you in. Maybe it says "Wrong password," maybe it says "We couldn't verify it's you," or maybe it just spins forever on the loading screen.

Being locked out of your email is stressful — your email is the key to everything else (banking, social media, online shopping, work). The problem is usually NOT that you forgot your password. It's something else: an old saved password your browser keeps filling in, a two-factor authentication glitch, a locked account, or a browser that's holding onto outdated login data. This guide covers all the reasons you can't sign in — even when you're sure your password is correct.

**What you'll need:**
- Your email address and password
- Access to your recovery phone or email (in case you need to verify your identity)
- A phone or computer with internet access
- About 15 minutes

> **If you truly forgot your password:** This guide covers sign-in problems when you KNOW your password. If you actually forgot it, use the "How to Recover a Forgotten Email Password" guide on this site instead.

## Step 1: Check the Obvious (But Easily Missed) Things

Before diving into settings and account recovery, rule out the simple stuff. Most "can't sign in" problems are caused by one of these five issues:

**Fix 1 — Check these five things, in order**

1. **Caps Lock:** Look at your keyboard. Is there a light on for Caps Lock? On phones, check if the shift key (⇧) is highlighted. Passwords are case-sensitive — "Password123" and "password123" are completely different. Turn Caps Lock OFF and try again

2. **Typing in the right field:** This sounds silly, but many login pages have two fields: one for your email/username and one for your password. Make sure you're typing your password in the PASSWORD field, not the email field. It happens more than you'd think

3. **Extra spaces:** When you copy your password from somewhere (a notes app, a text message), the copy often grabs an invisible space at the beginning or end. Click into the password field, press **Backspace/Delete** a few extra times at the beginning AND the end, and retype the last character manually

4. **Wrong email address:** If you have multiple email accounts (like a Gmail and a Yahoo), make sure you're typing the right one. Also check the domain: `@gmail.com` vs `@googlemail.com` (both work for Gmail, but only if you use the one you signed up with), `@outlook.com` vs `@hotmail.com` vs `@live.com`

5. **Browser auto-fill is inserting an OLD password:** Your web browser saves passwords and automatically fills them in. But if you changed your password recently, the browser might be filling in the OLD one. Look at the password field — if it's already filled with dots, delete EVERYTHING and manually type your current password

> **Common mistake:** People rely entirely on their browser's saved passwords and never actually type their password. When the saved password is outdated (changed on another device), the browser fills in the old one, and sign-in fails. The person thinks "I know my password is right!" but the browser is inserting the wrong one. Always manually type your password once to rule this out.

## Step 2: Try a Different Browser, Device, or App

If you're 100% sure your password is correct but sign-in still fails, the problem might be your browser, your device, or the app you're using — not your account.

**Fix 2 — Sign in using a different method**

Try these in order until one works:

1. **Try a different web browser:** If you're using Chrome, try Firefox or Safari. If you're on an iPhone, try both Safari AND Chrome. Some browsers have extensions (ad blockers, privacy tools) that interfere with sign-in pages
2. **Try a private/incognito window:** This loads the sign-in page without any saved cookies, cache, or extensions. In Chrome, press **Ctrl+Shift+N** (Windows) or **Cmd+Shift+N** (Mac). In Safari, go to **File → New Private Window**. In Firefox, press **Ctrl+Shift+P** (Windows) or **Cmd+Shift+P** (Mac). Try signing in here
3. **Try a different device entirely:** If you're locked out on your laptop, try signing in on your phone's web browser. If the phone works but the laptop doesn't, the problem is with your laptop — not your account
4. **Try the email provider's official app:** Download the Gmail app, Yahoo Mail app, or Outlook app on your phone. Apps often use a different sign-in method than websites and can bypass browser-related issues
5. **Try using mobile data instead of Wi-Fi:** Some networks (workplaces, schools, public Wi-Fi) block certain sign-in pages. Turn off Wi-Fi on your phone and try signing in using cellular data

> **Tip:** If signing in works on one device but not another, the broken device likely has a browser issue. Clear the browser's cache and cookies on that device: go to the browser's Settings/Preferences → Privacy & Security → Clear browsing data → select "Cookies" and "Cached images/files" → Clear data. Then try again.

## Step 3: Fix "Wrong Password" When You're Sure It's Right

This is the most maddening scenario: you KNOW your password is correct, but the website keeps saying it's wrong. Here are the specific causes — and fixes — for each major email provider.

**Fix 3 — Gmail says "Wrong password" but you know it's right**

1. First, go to **myaccount.google.com** in your browser. If you're already signed into ANY Google service (YouTube, Google Maps, Google Drive) on this device, you might be able to access your account settings without entering your password again
2. If you get in, go to **Security → Password** and change your password to something new. Even if you think the old one is correct, just change it — this resets any account locks and forces a fresh login
3. If Gmail requires an **"App Password"** for your device: go to **myaccount.google.com/apppasswords**, sign in, select "Mail" and your device type, and use the 16-character password Google generates. This is required if you have 2-Step Verification turned on and you're signing in on an older device or app

**Fix 4 — Yahoo Mail says "Invalid password" repeatedly**

Yahoo sometimes locks accounts after too many failed sign-in attempts — even if the LAST attempt was correct. The lock can last from 1 hour to 24 hours.

1. Wait 12 hours without attempting to sign in. Yes, really. Each failed attempt resets the lock timer
2. After waiting, go to **mail.yahoo.com** in a private/incognito browser window
3. Try signing in once. If it fails, use the **"Forgot password?"** link to reset your password — this often bypasses the lock

**Fix 5 — Outlook/Hotmail says "That password is incorrect"**

Microsoft accounts have a feature called "passwordless" sign-in. If you enabled it, your regular password won't work anymore — you must use the Microsoft Authenticator app or a code sent to your phone.

1. On the sign-in screen, look for **"Sign in with Microsoft Authenticator"** or **"Use a security key instead"** links below the password field
2. If you see **"Send a code to [your phone number]"**, click that and sign in with the code instead of your password
3. Once signed in, go to **account.microsoft.com/security** → **Advanced security options** → turn OFF "Passwordless account" if you prefer using a regular password

> **Warning:** If you repeatedly try the same wrong password (or even the right password during an account lock), some providers will lock your account for increasingly long periods — from 1 hour, to 24 hours, to a full week. If you've tried more than 5 times, STOP. Wait 24 hours before trying again, and use the password reset option instead of guessing.

## Step 4: Fix Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Problems

Two-factor authentication (also called 2-Step Verification or Two-Step Login) adds an extra layer of security. After entering your password, you must also enter a code from your phone. But what if you can't get the code?

**Fix 6 — Not receiving the 2FA code**

1. **Check the time on your phone.** If you use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, Authy), the codes are time-based. If your phone's clock is wrong (even by a minute), the codes won't work. Go to your phone's **Settings → General → Date & Time** and turn ON **"Set Automatically"**
2. **Check if the code went to a different device.** If you have multiple phones, tablets, or computers, the 2FA prompt might have gone to your iPad while you're staring at your iPhone. Check all your devices
3. **Try the backup code.** When you set up 2FA, your email provider gave you a set of backup codes (usually 8-10 codes, each one-time use). Search your email for "backup codes" or check your password manager. Enter one of these codes instead of the 2FA code
4. **Use a different 2FA method.** Most providers let you choose how to get your code:
   - Text message (SMS) to your phone
   - Phone call with an automated voice reading the code
   - Authenticator app
   - Backup codes
   - Tap "Yes" on a prompt sent to your other signed-in devices
   
   On the sign-in screen, look for **"Try another way"** or **"More options"** and try each method

5. **Check if your phone number changed.** If you set up 2FA with an old phone number you no longer have, you're in a tough spot. Go to your provider's account recovery page (accounts.google.com/signin/recovery for Gmail, login.yahoo.com/forgot for Yahoo, account.live.com/password/reset for Microsoft) and look for an option to recover without your phone

> **Common mistake:** People enable 2FA, save their backup codes in their email account, and then get locked out of that email. You can't access backup codes saved IN the account you're locked out of. Always save backup codes somewhere outside your email: a printed piece of paper in your desk drawer, a password manager, or a note on your phone's built-in Notes app.

## Step 5: Fix "Your Account Has Been Locked" Messages

Email providers lock accounts when they detect suspicious activity — too many wrong password attempts, sign-ins from unusual locations, or signs that someone else might be trying to break in. An account lock is scary but it's actually a GOOD thing: it means your provider's security systems are protecting you.

**Fix 7 — Unlock a locked Gmail account**

1. Go to **accounts.google.com/signin/recovery**
2. Enter your email address and click **Next**
3. Google will guide you through identity verification. You'll need:
   - Your recovery phone number or email
   - Or to answer security questions
   - Or to confirm your identity from a device you've previously used to sign in
4. Once verified, Google will unlock your account. This usually takes less than 5 minutes
5. After unlocking, change your password immediately — even if you think the old one was fine

**Fix 8 — Unlock a locked Yahoo account**

1. Go to **login.yahoo.com/forgot**
2. Enter your email address and click **Continue**
3. Yahoo will send a verification code to your recovery phone or email
4. Enter the code, answer any security questions, and Yahoo will unlock your account
5. Yahoo may also ask you to change your password as part of the unlock process

**Fix 9 — Unlock a locked Microsoft/Outlook account**

1. Go to **account.live.com/password/reset**
2. Enter your email and the CAPTCHA. Click **Next**
3. Verify your identity using your recovery email or phone
4. Once verified, Microsoft will let you sign in. Your account is now unlocked

> **Warning:** If you see a message like "Your account has been locked for security reasons" and you DIDN'T do anything unusual (no multiple wrong password attempts, no travel to a new location), someone else may be trying to break into your account. After unlocking, immediately change your password AND enable 2FA if you haven't already. Also check your account's recent activity for unfamiliar sign-ins.

## Step 6: Clear Browser Cache, Cookies, and Saved Passwords

Sometimes your browser stores corrupted data that breaks the sign-in process. The login page loads wrong, or it keeps submitting an old session that no longer works.

**Fix 10 — Clear browser data**

**In Chrome (computer):**
1. Press **Ctrl+Shift+Delete** (Windows) or **Cmd+Shift+Delete** (Mac)
2. Set **Time range** to **"All time"**
3. Check all three: **Cookies and other site data**, **Cached images and files**, and **Passwords and other sign-in data**
4. Click **Clear data**
5. Close Chrome completely, reopen it, and try signing in again

**In Safari (Mac):**
1. Click **Safari** in the top menu bar → **Settings** (or **Preferences**)
2. Go to the **Privacy** tab → click **Manage Website Data**
3. Search for your email provider (e.g., "google," "yahoo," "outlook") in the search bar
4. Select the matching entries and click **Remove** (or **Remove All** to be thorough)
5. Close Safari, reopen it, and try again

**On iPhone/iPad (Safari):**
1. Open **Settings** → **Safari** → scroll down and tap **Clear History and Website Data**
2. Tap **Clear History and Data** to confirm
3. This signs you out of all websites. Try signing into your email again

**On Android (Chrome):**
1. Open Chrome and tap the **three dots (⋮)** → **History** → **Clear browsing data**
2. Set **Time range** to **"All time"**
3. Check **Cookies and site data** and **Cached images and files**
4. Tap **Clear data**
5. Close Chrome and reopen it

> **Common mistake:** People clear their browser cache and then panic because they're signed out of EVERYTHING — Netflix, Amazon, social media, and every other website. Clearing cookies signs you out of all websites. This is normal and expected. You'll need to sign back into each website, but your accounts and data are all still there. Have your important passwords ready before you clear cookies.

## Still Stuck?

- **"Too many failed attempts — try again in X hours"?** The waiting period is real and cannot be bypassed. Do NOT keep trying — each attempt resets the timer. Set an alarm on your phone for when the lock expires, and try ONCE on a device and location you've used before. If it fails again, use the password reset option instead.
- **Sign-in works on one computer but not another?** The problem computer likely has a browser issue (see Step 6) or its clock is wrong. Check the date and time on the taskbar/menu bar. If your computer's clock is off by more than a few minutes, security certificates fail and sign-in breaks. On Windows: right-click the clock → Adjust date/time → toggle "Set time automatically" ON. On Mac: System Settings → General → Date & Time → toggle "Set date and time automatically" ON.
- **"Your account has been disabled/deleted"?** This is serious and different from a lock. Google deletes accounts that have been inactive for 2+ years. Yahoo deletes accounts after 12 months of inactivity. Microsoft deletes accounts after 2 years of inactivity. If your account was deleted, it's almost always permanent — you cannot recover it. You'll need to create a new account.
- **Using a VPN or traveling abroad?** Sign-in pages sometimes block logins from countries you don't normally visit. If you're using a VPN, turn it OFF and try again. If you're traveling, your provider may require additional verification (like answering security questions) to confirm it's really you signing in from a new country.
- **Nothing works — what's my last resort?** If you've exhausted every option, create a new email account. Use the "How to Create a Gmail Account" guide on this site. Then, as painful as it is, you'll need to update your email address on every service that used the old one (bank, shopping, social media, etc.). To prevent this from happening again, immediately set up recovery options and enable 2FA on your new account — and save the backup codes somewhere safe.
