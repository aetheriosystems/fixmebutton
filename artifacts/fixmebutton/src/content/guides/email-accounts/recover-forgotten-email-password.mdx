## Before You Start

You're staring at the login screen. You've tried every password you can think of — your pet's name, your birthday, that one password you use for everything — and none of them work. Your email account is locked and you can't get in.

This is scary, but you are NOT permanently locked out. Every major email provider (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook, iCloud) has a password recovery process. As long as you set up recovery options (a backup email or phone number) when you created the account, you can get back in within minutes.

This guide covers the recovery process for the four most popular email providers. Jump to the section that matches your email address.

**What you'll need:**
- Access to the phone number or recovery email you used when you created the account
- A phone or computer with internet access
- About 10 minutes

> **Don't skip the "Before You're Locked Out" section at the end.** Setting up recovery options NOW — while you still have access — makes future recovery instant instead of impossible.

## Step 1: Recover a Gmail / Google Account Password

Gmail accounts use Google's account recovery system. The process works best if you have a recovery phone number or email on file, but Google also offers alternative verification methods.

**Fix 1 — Use Google's Account Recovery**

1. Go to **accounts.google.com/signin/recovery** in your web browser
2. Enter your full Gmail address (including `@gmail.com`) and click **Next**
3. Google will ask: **"Enter the last password you remember"**. If you don't remember any password, click **"Try another way"** at the bottom
4. Google will now try to verify it's really you. The options depend on what recovery info you added to your account:
   - **Recovery phone number:** Google sends a 6-digit code via text or phone call. Enter the code you receive
   - **Recovery email:** Google sends a verification code to your backup email address. Check that inbox, copy the code, and paste it into the recovery page
   - **Authenticator app:** If you set up Google Authenticator, open the app and enter the 6-digit code shown for your Google account
   - **Security question:** Google may ask a question you set up previously (like "What was your first pet's name?")
5. Once verified, you'll be taken to a page where you can **create a new password**
6. Type a new password (at least 8 characters), type it again to confirm, and click **Save password**
7. You'll be signed into your Gmail account. Write down your new password somewhere safe!

> **Warning:** If you don't have access to your recovery phone or email, Google's recovery becomes much harder. Google may ask you to enter a code sent to the same device you previously used to sign in, or ask you to wait 3-5 days before trying again. There is NO phone support for free Gmail accounts — you cannot call Google to recover your password.

> **Tip:** If Google says it "couldn't verify that this account belongs to you," try again from a device and location you've used before to sign in. Google uses your sign-in history as an additional proof of ownership. Recovery is more likely to succeed from your home Wi-Fi on your regular phone or laptop than from a public computer at a library.

## Step 2: Recover a Yahoo Mail Password

Yahoo's recovery process is similar to Google's, but Yahoo also offers phone support for paid accounts and has a slightly different flow.

**Fix 2 — Use Yahoo's Account Recovery**

1. Go to **login.yahoo.com/forgot** in your web browser
2. Enter your full Yahoo email address (including `@yahoo.com`) and click **Continue**
3. Yahoo will offer to send a verification code. The options are:
   - **Text message** to your recovery phone number
   - **Email** to your recovery email address
4. Choose the method you can access and click **Yes, send me a verification code**
5. Check your phone or recovery email for the code. It's usually 8 digits for Yahoo
6. Enter the code on the recovery page and click **Verify**
7. Yahoo will now let you create a new password. Type a new password (at least 8 characters with letters, numbers, and symbols), confirm it, and click **Continue**
8. You're now signed into Yahoo Mail

> **Common mistake:** People enter their Yahoo email but forget they have a recovery email that's ALSO a Yahoo address. If your recovery email is another Yahoo account that you're also locked out of, you won't get the code. When choosing the recovery method, always pick the option you can actually access right now. A phone number is usually safer than a recovery email for this reason.

> **Tip:** If you don't have access to either your recovery phone or email, click **"I don't have access to this phone"** or **"I don't have access to this email"** on the verification screen. Yahoo will then ask you to answer your security questions (if you set them up). If you can't answer those either, Yahoo paid subscribers can contact phone support. Free account users may not have additional recovery options.

## Step 3: Recover an Outlook / Hotmail / Microsoft Account Password

Microsoft accounts cover Outlook.com, Hotmail.com, Live.com, and MSN.com email addresses. The recovery process is unified through Microsoft's account system.

**Fix 3 — Use Microsoft's Account Recovery**

1. Go to **account.live.com/password/reset** in your web browser
2. Select **"I forgot my password"** and click **Next**
3. Enter your full email address (outlook.com, hotmail.com, etc.) and the CAPTCHA characters shown on screen. Click **Next**
4. Microsoft needs to verify your identity. Choose how to receive your security code:
   - **Email** to your recovery email address
   - **Text** to your recovery phone number
   - **Authenticator app** (if you set up Microsoft Authenticator)
5. Click **Send code**. Check your phone or recovery email for the code
6. Enter the code and click **Next**
7. Create a new password (at least 8 characters, with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols). Type it twice to confirm
8. Click **Next**. You'll see a confirmation that your password has been reset
9. Click **Sign in** and log in with your new password

> **Common mistake:** Microsoft's CAPTCHA asks you to type the characters you see in a distorted image. If you get it wrong, it gives you a new image. People sometimes get stuck in a loop here. The CAPTCHA is case-sensitive, and some characters (like 0 vs O, or l vs 1 vs I) can be confusing. If you get it wrong twice, click the **"New"** link or the **speaker icon** to hear an audio version instead.

## Step 4: Recover an iCloud / Apple ID Email Password

If your email ends in `@icloud.com`, `@me.com`, or `@mac.com`, it's an Apple account. The recovery process goes through Apple's system, which is different from the others.

**Fix 4 — Reset your Apple ID password**

**If you have your iPhone, iPad, or Mac with you:**

1. **On iPhone/iPad:** Open **Settings** → tap your name at the top → **Sign-In & Security** → **Change Password**
2. **On Mac:** Click the **Apple menu ()** → **System Settings** → your name at the top → **Sign-In & Security** → **Change Password**
3. Enter your device passcode (the one you use to unlock your phone or computer)
4. Enter a new password, confirm it, and tap **Change**
5. Your Apple ID password is now updated, and your iCloud email will work with the new password

**If you don't have your Apple device with you:**

1. Go to **iforgot.apple.com** in any web browser
2. Enter your Apple ID (your iCloud email address) and the CAPTCHA. Click **Continue**
3. Enter your **trusted phone number** (the phone number linked to your Apple ID). Click **Continue**
4. Apple will send a notification to all your Apple devices, plus a text message to your phone number
5. On your Apple device, tap **Allow** on the notification → follow the on-screen instructions
6. If you don't have an Apple device, use the code sent to your phone number instead
7. Enter the verification code on the website
8. Create a new password and click **Reset Password**

> **Warning:** Apple's account recovery can take DAYS if you don't have access to your trusted phone number. Apple calls this "Account Recovery" and it involves a waiting period (anywhere from a few days to two weeks) where Apple verifies your identity through other means before letting you reset. If the website says "Account recovery will take several days," there's no way to speed it up. The waiting period exists to prevent hackers from stealing your account.

> **Tip:** The fastest way to reset an Apple ID password is from a device that's already signed into your Apple ID. The device passcode is all you need — no email codes, no phone verification. If you're locked out on every device, recovery through iforgot.apple.com is your only option.

## Before You're Locked Out: Set Up Recovery Options Now

Don't wait until you're locked out. Taking 5 minutes now saves hours (or days) of frustration later.

**For any email account, do these three things:**

1. **Add a recovery phone number:** Go to your account settings → Security → add your current mobile number. This is the single most important recovery step
2. **Add a recovery email:** Add a second email address you can access. It can be from a different provider (e.g., if your main email is Gmail, add your work email or your spouse's email as the recovery address)
3. **Write your password in a password manager or a notebook:** Use a password manager (like the one built into your iPhone or Chrome) OR write it in a physical notebook kept in a safe place. Do NOT store it in a Notes app on your phone — if you lose your phone, you lose the note

| Email Provider | Where to Set Up Recovery |
|---|---|
| Gmail | myaccount.google.com/security → "How you sign in to Google" |
| Yahoo | mail.yahoo.com → Settings → Account Security |
| Outlook/Hotmail | account.microsoft.com/security → "Advanced security options" |
| iCloud/Apple | Settings → your name → Sign-In & Security → Account Recovery |

> **Common mistake:** People use a recovery phone number that's no longer active (an old number they switched away from) or a recovery email they can't access anymore (an old work email from a job they left). Check your recovery settings every 6 months and update them if anything changed. If you switch phone carriers but keep the same number, you're fine. But if you get a NEW phone number, update it in your account security settings immediately.

## Still Stuck?

- **None of the recovery methods work?** If you absolutely cannot recover your account, your last option is to create a new email address and move on. Use the "How to Create a Gmail Account" guide on this site to get started. Then, notify your contacts of your new address.
- **Recovery says "try again later"?** Google, Yahoo, and Microsoft all impose waiting periods if you attempt recovery too many times in a short period. Wait 24 hours and try again from a device and location you've used before.
- **Worried someone else is trying to break into your account?** If you received a recovery code or password reset email that you didn't request, someone else may be trying to access your account. Do NOT click any links in that email. Instead, go directly to your provider's website (type the address yourself) and change your password immediately. Also enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for extra security.
- **Using a work or school email?** Contact your IT department directly. They control your account and can reset your password from their admin panel. The self-service recovery options in this guide only work for personal email accounts.
