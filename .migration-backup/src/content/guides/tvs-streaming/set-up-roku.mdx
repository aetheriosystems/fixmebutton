## Before You Start

Setting up a Roku turns any TV into a smart TV with Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, YouTube, and hundreds of other channels. This guide works for all Roku models.

**What you'll need:**
- Your Roku device
- The included remote (with batteries)
- The included HDMI cable (or the Roku stick itself plugs directly into HDMI)
- The included USB power cable and power adapter
- Your TV's remote (you'll need it to switch inputs)
- Your WiFi network name and password

## Step 1: Plug the Roku Into Your TV

**For Roku Streaming Stick (plugs directly into TV):**
1. Find an available HDMI port on the back or side of your TV. HDMI ports are rectangular, about ¾ inch wide.
2. Plug the Roku stick directly into the HDMI port. The Roku logo should face up.
3. If the stick doesn't fit because of tight space, use the included HDMI extender cable.

**For Roku Express or Ultra (uses HDMI cable):**
1. Plug one end of the HDMI cable into the Roku box.
2. Plug the other end into an HDMI port on your TV.

## Step 2: Connect the Power

1. Plug the small end of the USB power cable into the side of your Roku.
2. Plug the larger end (USB-A) into the included power adapter.
3. Plug the power adapter into a wall outlet.

> **Important:** Always use the wall adapter that came with your Roku. Plugging into your TV's USB port may not provide enough power and can cause glitches.

A white light should appear on your Roku when it's powered on.

## Step 3: Switch Your TV to the Right Input

1. Grab your **TV remote** (not the Roku remote).
2. Press the **Input**, **Source**, or **TV/Video** button. The button name varies by TV brand.
3. You'll see a list of inputs — HDMI 1, HDMI 2, HDMI 3, etc.
4. Select the HDMI port you plugged the Roku into.
5. You should see the Roku welcome screen with a bouncing Roku logo. If you don't, try each HDMI input one by one.

## Step 4: Pair the Roku Remote

1. Open the battery cover on the back of the Roku remote and insert the included batteries.
2. The remote should pair automatically. You'll see a message on screen when it's connected.
3. If it doesn't pair automatically: press and hold the small button inside the battery compartment for 5 seconds. The pairing light on the remote will flash green.

## Step 5: Choose Your Language and Connect to WiFi

1. Use the arrow keys on the Roku remote to select your **Language**, then press **OK** (the center button).
2. Select **Set up for home use** (not "store mode").
3. Select your **WiFi network** from the list.
4. Enter your WiFi password using the on-screen keyboard. Use the arrow keys to move and press OK to type each character.
5. Select **Connect**. Your Roku will test the connection and may download updates — this can take a few minutes.

## Step 6: Create or Sign In to Your Roku Account

A Roku account is required and free.

1. The TV will display a code — something like "XD42G" — and a website: **roku.com/link**
2. On your phone or computer, go to roku.com/link
3. Enter the code shown on your TV screen
4. Sign in with an existing Roku account, or create a new one (free — you don't need to enter payment info if you skip that step)

> **Tip:** When creating an account, Roku will ask for a payment method. Look for a small "Skip" or "Remind Me Later" link if you don't want to add one yet.

## Step 7: Set Up Your Channels

After signing in, your Roku will walk you through adding popular channels (apps):

1. Roku will suggest popular channels like Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, YouTube, and Prime Video
2. Select the ones you use by pressing **OK**. A checkmark appears on selected channels.
3. When done, select **Continue**
4. The channels will download and install automatically

You can always add more channels later from the Roku home screen by selecting **Streaming Channels** from the left menu.

## Step 8: Complete Setup and Start Watching

1. Follow any remaining on-screen prompts to finish setup
2. You'll land on the Roku home screen — a grid of your installed channels
3. Open a channel like Netflix or YouTube
4. Sign in with your account for that service (Netflix login, Google account for YouTube, etc.)
5. Start watching!

## Still Stuck?

- **Remote not working?** Try fresh batteries. If the remote still doesn't respond, download the free Roku mobile app (iOS/Android) which includes a remote control.
- **WiFi keeps disconnecting?** Move your router closer to the TV, or consider a Roku model with an Ethernet port (like Roku Ultra) for a wired connection.
- **Can't find an HDMI port?** Older TVs may not have HDMI. You'll need an HDMI-to-composite converter (about $15-20 online) or a Roku Express+ model that supports older TVs.
- **Forgot your Roku account password?** Go to my.roku.com and click "Forgot password?"
