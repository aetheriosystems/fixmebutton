## Before You Start

A slow computer is frustrating, but most slowdowns are fixable without buying anything. This guide covers both Windows and Mac. Skip to the sections that match your computer.

## Step 1: Restart Your Computer

The simplest fix that works surprisingly often. Restarting clears temporary files, stops stuck background programs, and refreshes memory.

- **Windows**: Click the Start button → Power icon → Restart
- **Mac**: Click the Apple menu (top-left corner) → Restart

Wait for the computer to fully restart and try using it again. Is it faster?

## Step 2: Close Unnecessary Programs

Every open program uses memory and processing power. Many people leave dozens of browser tabs and programs open.

- **Windows**: Look at your taskbar (bottom of screen). Right-click any program icon you're not using and select **Close window**
- **Mac**: Look at your Dock. Right-click (or two-finger click) any program icon you're not using and select **Quit**

Especially close web browser tabs you're not actively using. Each tab uses memory — having 50 tabs open can dramatically slow down an older computer.

## Step 3: Disable Startup Programs

Many programs automatically launch when you turn on your computer, slowing down startup and running in the background.

**Windows:**
1. Press **Ctrl + Shift + Esc** to open Task Manager
2. Click the **Startup** tab (you may need to click "More details" first)
3. You'll see a list of programs with their "Startup impact" — look for anything marked "High"
4. Right-click any program you don't need at startup and select **Disable**

Common culprits: Spotify, Steam, Skype, OneDrive, Adobe, and printer software.

**Mac:**
1. Click the Apple menu → **System Settings** → **General** → **Login Items & Extensions**
2. Look under "Open at Login"
3. Select any program you don't need at startup and click the **minus (-)** button

## Step 4: Free Up Disk Space

When your hard drive is nearly full, your computer slows down significantly.

**Windows:**
1. Open **Settings** (gear icon in Start menu)
2. Click **System** → **Storage**
3. Turn on **Storage Sense** — this automatically cleans up temporary files
4. Click **Temporary files** and select which ones to delete. It's safe to check everything here.
5. Click **Remove files**

**Mac:**
1. Click the Apple menu → **System Settings** → **General** → **Storage**
2. Review the recommendations at the top
3. Click the **i** icon next to categories like Documents, Messages, and Applications
4. Delete large files you no longer need — especially downloads, old iPhone backups, and large videos

> **Aim for at least 20 GB of free space.** If you're below 10 GB, that's likely why your computer is slow.

## Step 5: Check for Malware (Windows)

Unwanted programs and browser extensions can slow down your computer.

1. Open **Windows Security** (search for it in the Start menu)
2. Click **Virus & threat protection**
3. Click **Quick scan**
4. If anything is found, follow the prompts to remove it

Also check your browser extensions:
1. In Chrome/Edge: click the puzzle piece icon (top right) → **Manage extensions**
2. Remove any extensions you don't recognize or don't use

## Step 6: Update Your Operating System

Updates often include performance fixes.

**Windows:**
1. Open **Settings** → **Windows Update**
2. Click **Check for updates**
3. Install any available updates and restart

**Mac:**
1. Click Apple menu → **System Settings** → **General** → **Software Update**
2. If an update is available, click **Update Now**

## Step 7: Consider Hardware Upgrades

If your computer is still slow after all the software fixes, the hardware might need help.

**Most impactful upgrades (in order):**

1. **Replace hard drive with SSD**: If your computer has a traditional spinning hard drive (HDD), replacing it with a Solid State Drive (SSD) is the single biggest speed upgrade possible. An SSD can make a 5-year-old computer feel brand new. Cost: $30-60 for a 500 GB SSD. Most computer repair shops can do this for you.

2. **Add more RAM**: If you have 4 GB or 8 GB of RAM and regularly run multiple programs, upgrading to 16 GB can help. Cost: $25-50.

3. **Clean the fans**: Over time, dust builds up inside computers and causes them to overheat, which makes them slow down as a safety measure. A computer repair shop can clean it, or you can carefully use compressed air.

## Still Stuck?

- If your computer is more than 6-7 years old, it may simply be reaching the end of its useful life for modern software. Consider upgrading to a newer model.
- For Windows: you can try **resetting Windows** (Settings → System → Recovery → Reset this PC). Choose "Keep my files" to preserve your documents.
- For Mac: try **reinstalling macOS** (restart and hold Command+R → select Reinstall macOS). This keeps your files but refreshes the operating system.
