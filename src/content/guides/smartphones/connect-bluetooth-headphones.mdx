## Before You Start

Connecting Bluetooth headphones is the same basic process for almost all brands — AirPods, Beats, Sony, Bose, JBL, and generic brands all work the same way.

**What you'll need:**
- Your Bluetooth headphones (charged)
- Your phone

## Step 1: Put Your Headphones in Pairing Mode

This is the most common point of confusion. Every headphone brand has a slightly different way to enter pairing mode:

**AirPods / Beats:**
1. Put both earbuds in the charging case
2. Open the lid (keep earbuds inside)
3. Press and hold the small button on the **back** of the case until the light flashes **white**

**Most other headphones (Sony, Bose, JBL, etc.):**
1. Turn the headphones off
2. Press and hold the **power button** for 5-10 seconds — longer than just turning them on
3. You'll hear a voice say "pairing" or "Bluetooth pairing," and a light will flash blue and red alternately

> **Not sure?** Search online for "[your headphone brand] pairing mode" — every brand has a specific method. The key sign is a **flashing light** (usually blue, or alternating blue/red).

## Step 2: Open Your Phone's Bluetooth Settings

**iPhone:**
1. Open the **Settings** app (gray gear icon)
2. Tap **Bluetooth**
3. Make sure the switch at the top is **green** (on)

**Android:**
1. Swipe down from the top of your screen twice to open Quick Settings
2. Look for the **Bluetooth** icon (looks like a jagged "B") — tap it to turn it on
3. Or: go to **Settings → Connections → Bluetooth**

## Step 3: Find Your Headphones in the List

Under "Other Devices" or "Available Devices," you should see your headphones appear by name — for example, "AirPods Pro," "WH-1000XM5," or "JBL Tune."

If you don't see them:
- Make sure the headphones are still in pairing mode (flashing light)
- Turn Bluetooth off and back on
- Move the headphones closer to your phone (within 3 feet)

## Step 4: Tap to Connect

1. Tap the name of your headphones in the list
2. Wait a few seconds. You might see "Connecting..." then "Connected"
3. Once connected, you'll see "Connected" next to the headphones name

On iPhone with AirPods, you'll also see a battery popup showing the charge level of both the case and earbuds.

## Step 5: Test the Connection

1. Open any app with sound — YouTube, Spotify, or just play a video
2. The sound should come through your headphones now
3. Try adjusting the volume using your phone's volume buttons

> **Troubleshooting:** If there's no sound, check that the volume is turned up on BOTH your phone AND your headphones (many headphones have their own volume control).

## Still Stuck?

- **Headphones won't enter pairing mode?** Make sure they're charged. Many headphones won't enter pairing mode if the battery is too low.
- **Already connected to another device?** Bluetooth headphones can only connect to ONE device at a time. Turn off Bluetooth on your laptop, tablet, or any other device they might be connected to.
- **"Connection Failed" or "Pairing Unsuccessful"?** On your phone, tap the "i" or gear icon next to the headphones name and choose **Forget This Device**. Then start from Step 1 again.
- **Still not working?** Try connecting the headphones to a different phone or computer. If they don't work there either, the headphones may need to be reset. Search for "[headphone brand] factory reset" for instructions.
